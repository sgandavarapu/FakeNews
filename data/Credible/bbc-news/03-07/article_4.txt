Regrettably, those mistakes mean that we have not been able to provide the complete set of records that we should have around the specific race relevant to Ukad's investigation. We accept full responsibility for this.</p><p>\"However, many of the subsequent assumptions and assertions about the way Team Sky operates have been inaccurate or extended to implications that are simply untrue.</p><p>\"Our commitment to anti-doping has been a core principle of Team Sky since its inception. Our mission is to race and win clean, and we have done so for eight years.</p><p>\"To my understanding, Ukad's extensive investigation has found nothing whatsoever to support this allegation, which we believe to be false.</p><p>\"Some of the comments made about Team Sky have been unreasonable and incorrect.\"</p><h3 class=\"sp-story-body__cross-head\">How did we get here?</h3><p><b>September 2016</b></p><ul><li>Wiggins and Team Sky come under scrutiny for his use of TUEs after his confidential medical information was leaked by hackers 'Fancy Bears'.</li><li>Wiggins, an asthma and allergy sufferer, received special permission to use triamcinolone shortly before the 2012 Tour de France as well as the previous year's event and the 2013 Giro d'Italia.</li><li>His TUEs were approved by British authorities, and cycling's world governing body the UCI. There is no suggestion either the 36-year-old or Team Sky broke any rules.</li></ul><p><b>October 2016</b></p><ul><li>A Daily Mail investigation revealed Team Sky and Wiggins were being investigated by Ukad over the contents of the 'mystery package'.</li><li>Ukad officials visited British Cycling headquarters in Manchester as part of a investigation into allegations of wrongdoing in the sport.</li></ul><p><b>December 2016</b></p><ul><li>Brailsford faced the Department for Culture, Media and Sport Committee (DCMS) hearing into doping in sport. He told MPs Freeman had told him the package contained Fluimucil.</li></ul><p><b>January 2017</b></p><ul><li>Ex-Olympic champion Nicole Cooke tells MPs she is \"sceptical\" of Team Sky's drug-free credentials and Wiggins' TUEs.</li></ul><p><b>March 2017</b></p><ul><li>Ukad chief Nicole Sapstead tells MPs Freeman, who received the package, has no record of his medical treatment at the time.</li><li>Freeman, who missed the select committee hearing on 2 March because of ill health, had a laptop containing medical records stolen.</li></ul><h3 class=\"sp-story-body__cross-head\">Support for Brailsford, questions for Wiggins</h3><p>Moments after the letter and document were published, Team Sky board chairman Graham McWilliam <a class=\"sp-story-body__external-link\" href=\"https://twitter.com/grahammcwilliam/status/839164354209673217\">tweeted his \"100%\" support for Brailsford,<i class=\"sp-story-body__external-link-icon gelicon gelicon--external\" aria-hidden=\"true\"></i></a> saying the board were 100% behind the team principal and looking forward to \"many more years of success.\"</p><p>\"Pleased to see Team Sky challenging some of the inaccurate commentary of recent days,\" McWilliam added.</p><p>Britain's Geraint Thomas - <a href=\"/sport/cycling/39185863\">one of a majority of Team Sky riders to back control. </p><p>They will hope that the combination of contrition and defiance in this eight-page document, along with more detailed explanations of some of the questions raised by last week's
