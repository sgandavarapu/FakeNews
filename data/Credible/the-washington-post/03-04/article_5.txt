</div> </div> <p>Two days after the presidential election, a Russian official speaking to a reporter in Moscow offered a surprising acknowledgment: The Kremlin had been in contact with Donald Trump\u2019s campaign.</p> <p>The claim, coming amid allegations that Russia had interfered with the election, was met with an immediate no-wiggle-room, blanket denial from Trump\u2019s spokeswoman. \u201cIt never happened,\u201d Hope Hicks <a href=\"http://bigstory.ap.org/article/323f28f7f5e242498f43e4a7188336bc/trumps-election-boosts-kremlin-hopes-better-relations\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"bigstory.ap.org\">told the Associated Press</a> at the time. \u201cThere was no communication between the campaign and any foreign entity during the campaign.\u201d</p> <p>In fact, it is now clear it did happen.</p> <p>The past few days have brought a growing list of confirmed communications between Trump campaign aides and Russian officials, with each new revelation adding to a cloud of suspicion that hangs over the White House as critics demand an independent investigation.</p> <p>Trump\u2019s team has offered various explanations for the meetings: Some encounters, they have said, were brief, no more than casual, polite introductions. Others involved the routine diplomacy common for officials surrounding a candidate for the nation\u2019s highest office.</p> <p>Attorney General Jeff Sessions, who was an early Trump campaign adviser, said <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/sessions-spoke-twice-with-russian-ambassador-during-trumps-presidential-campaign-justice-officials-say/2017/03/01/77205eda-feac-11e6-99b4-9e613afeb09f_story.html\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">his two interactions</a> with Russian Ambassador Sergey Kislyak, first reported this week by The Washington Post, came in his role as a senator, not as a campaign surrogate.</p> <p>It is unclear why the White House has consistently denied contacts with Russian officials if the meetings that took place were innocuous.<b></b> </p> <p>As a result, the confirmations of the encounters have trickled out through a series of news stories that have proved increasingly damaging to the Trump administration, with some Trump associates appearing to shift their accounts over time.</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0.5625\" data-category-id=\"analysis\" data-duration=\"176512\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"What kinds of investigations could be launched into Russian election interference?\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\">(Peter Stevenson/The Washington Post)</span> </div> </div> <p>Already, Michael Flynn was <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/michael-flynn-resigns-as-national-security-adviser/2017/02/13/0007c0a8-f26e-11e6-8d72-263470bf0401_story.html?utm_term=.657c7a15cf98\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">forced to resign</a> as national security adviser as a result of his post-election contacts with Kislyak. This week, the White House confirmed that those conversations included a brief meeting alongside Trump\u2019s son-in-law and senior adviser, Jared Kushner, at Trump Tower in New York in December. White House spokeswoman Sarah Huckabee Sanders on Friday termed that discussion merely a \u201ccourtesy meeting.\u201d</p> <p>Sessions has now <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/powerpost/top-gop-lawmaker-calls-on-sessions-to-recuse-himself-from-russia-investigation/2017/03/02/148c07ac-ff46-11e6-8ebe-6e0dbe4f2bca_story.html?utm_term=.65ccbe6f184c\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">recused himself</a> from oversight of any investigation of Trump\u2019s ties to Moscow and is facing calls to step down as a result of his statement, during his January confirmation hearing, that he had not had any contacts with the Russians.</p> <p>On Friday, Sanders dismissed the brewing questions. \u201cThe big point here is the president himself knows what his involvement was, and that\u2019s zero,\u201d she said.</p> <p>Nevertheless, the recent revelations have made the post-<br align=\"block\"/>election comments from the Russian official newly relevant.</p> <p> <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/moscow-had-contacts-with-trump-team-during-campaign-russian-diplomat-says/2016/11/10/28fb82fa-a73d-11e6-9bd6-184ab22d218e_story.html?utm_term=.42e0f2ea8637\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">Those comments</a> came from Deputy Foreign Minister Sergei Ryabkov, who told the Interfax news agency in November that \u201cthere were contacts\u201d with Trump\u2019s aides.</p> <p>\u201cObviously,\u201d Ryabkov said, \u201cwe know most of the people from his entourage.\u201d</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-photo inline-photo-normal\"> <a name=\"\"></a> <img class=\"unprocessed\" data-hi-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_1484w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/03/03/National-Politics/Images/RussiaWeb.jpg?uuid=4fxxPgA9EeebeIJMyrlENQ\" data-low-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_480w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/03/03/National-Politics/Images/RussiaWeb.jpg?uuid=4fxxPgA9EeebeIJMyrlENQ\" </div> <p>As the Trump campaign rejected the assertion, other Russian officials said any communications would have been routine and offered to Democratic nominee Hillary Clinton\u2019s campaign as well \u2014 a contention denied at the time by a Clinton aide.</p> <p>Nearly all of the contacts that have emerged so far were with Kislyak, the affable Russian ambassador in Washington who is known as a consummate networker.</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/moscows-man-in-washington-is-at-the-center-of-the-political-moment/2017/03/02/7e624234-ff6b-11e6-99b4-9e613afeb09f_story.html?utm_term=.04d4d3eb3fa1\" shape=\"rect\">Moscow\u2019s man in Washington is at the center of the political moment</a>]</i> </p> <p>Kislyak appears to have worked to cultivate a relationship with the Trump campaign, starting his outreach even before Trump was thought likely to win the GOP nomination.</p> <p>In April, Kislyak popped up at the Mayflower Hotel, where he was seated in the front row at one of Trump\u2019s first major foreign policy addresses. During the speech, Trump offered a forceful promise that he would seek better relations with Russia.</p> <p>\u201cI believe an easing of tensions and improved relations with Russia \u2014 from a position of strength only \u2014 is possible, absolutely possible,\u201d he said.</p> <p id=\"U1200207180364FbH\">The event\u2019s host, Dimitri K. Simes, president of the Center for the National Interest, a foreign policy think tank, said Kislyak was one of four ambassadors who attended as guests of his group. Simes said he introduced Trump and Kislyak in a receiving line at a reception before the event, which was also attended by Sessions and Kushner, among other Trump aides.</p> <p>Simes, who is Russian American and favors warmer relations with Moscow, said it is common practice for foreign diplomats to try to get to know important advisers, like Sessions, to presidential candidates. \u201cLet me put it more bluntly: They would be derelict in their duty if they didn\u2019t try to get to know him,\u201d Simes said.</p> <p>Kislyak was also in attendance at the Republican National Convention, where he briefly <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/politics/wp/2017/03/02/what-jeff-sessions-said-about-russia-and-when/?utm_term=.7beee815f7de\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">met Sessions</a> after a July 18 Heritage Foundation event attended by dozens of diplomats.</p> <p>Two days later, Kislyak met with Trump advisers Carter Page and J.D. Gordon after a convention-related Global Partners in Diplomacy event at Case Western Reserve University.</p> <p>In an email, Gordon said he briefly spoke to Kislyak in a group of diplomats there and also at an evening reception. Gordon called it a \u201cbrief, informal conversation,\u201d during which he repeated public Trump statements about improving relations with Russia.</p> <p>Page also confirmed his interaction with Kislyak at the event to MSNBC on Thursday. Last month, he told PBS that he had held \u201cno meetings\u201d with any Russian officials during the campaign.</p> <p>Those meetings at the Republican convention came as questions about Trump\u2019s stance on Russia started to seriously enter campaign trail conversation.</p> <p>At the time, some GOP delegates were questioning the wording of the party platform and whether the Trump campaign had intervened to make it more Russia friendly. A delegate\u2019s proposal regarding U.S. support for Ukraine in its fight against Russia-backed rebels had been softened before inclusion in the document.</p> <p> </p> <p>Then-campaign manager Paul Manafort, who previously was <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-fix/wp/2016/08/19/paul-manaforts-complicated-ties-to-ukraine-explained/?utm_term=.078b91041a60\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">a paid adviser</a> to a pro-Russian Ukrainian president, told NBC in August that the change \u201cabsolutely did not come from the campaign.\u201d</p> <p>But this week, Gordon, the Trump campaign adviser, offered a different explanation. He said he had advocated for the change, believing it to match Trump\u2019s views. In an email, he said he had consulted about the matter with \u201ccampaign policy colleagues\u201d before arriving in Cleveland.</p> <p>Page\u2019s participation in the July meeting came 13 days after he drew scrutiny for a July 8 speech he delivered in Moscow in which he was critical of U.S. foreign policy.</p> <p>Page has denied holding substantive meetings with Russian officials during that trip but told The Post in September that he briefly met and shook hands with Russian Deputy Prime Minister Arkady Dvorkovich after his address. Page told MSNBC on Thursday that he held \u201cno material discussions\u201d during the trip.</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/economy/trump-advisers-public-comments-ties-to-moscow-stir-unease-in-both-parties/2016/08/05/2e8722fa-5815-11e6-9aee-8075993d73a2_story.html?utm_term=.04f3793793a6\" shape=\"rect\">Trump adviser Carter Page\u2019s public comments, ties to Moscow, stir unease in both parties </a>]</i> </p> <p>Russia\u2019s alleged interference in the U.S. election exploded as a public issue a few days after Page and Gordon met with Kislyak. On July 22, WikiLeaks posted thousands of emails hacked from the Democratic National Committee, causing recriminations in the party on the eve of its national convention.</p> <p>Rather than condemning the possible intervention in the election by a foreign power, Trump playfully called on the Russians to hack Clinton\u2019s private server and locate emails she had deleted. \u201cRussia, if you\u2019re listening, I hope you\u2019re able to find the 30,000 emails that are missing,\u201d <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/trump-invites-russia-to-meddle-in-the-us-presidential-race-with-clintons-emails/2016/07/27/a85d799e-5414-11e6-b7de-dfe509430c39_story.html?utm_term=.51a3c44901fc\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">he announced</a> at a July 27 news conference.</p> <p>Sessions met with Kislyak in his office on Capitol Hill on Sept.\u00a08, as U.S. officials were growing more concerned about Russia\u2019s role in the election. He said Thursday that the meeting came in his role as a member of the Senate Armed Services Committee, noting that it was one of many such meetings he took with diplomats.</p> <p>Sanders, the White House spokeswoman, told reporters Friday that suspicions about the meeting were \u201cpretty unfair\u201d given that Sessions\u2019s senatorial duties put him in touch routinely with diplomats.</p> <p>But a survey by The Post of all 26 members of the committee showed that Sessions was the only one to meet one-on-one with Kislyak in 2016. And a Polish diplomat <a href=\"http://wyborcza.pl/7,75399,21445779,niebezpieczne-zwiazki-ludzi-trumpa-z-kremlem-cd-nawet-republikanie.html?disableRedirects=true\" shape=\"rect\">who met with Sessions</a> in late spring said he did so at Sessions\u2019s request, at least in part because of Sessions\u2019s role with Trump.</p> <p>\u201cI was aware that Sessions was a senator and at the same time somebody close to Donald Trump. I just wanted to hear what he wanted to say \u2014 any message, communication, questions,\u201d said Ryszard Schnepf, who was ambassador at the time and has since retired. He declined to say what he and Sessions discussed, except to say the issues would have been \u201cof interest to a senator at the same time as [a surrogate] for somebody who is running for president.\u201d</p> <p>In a brief interview last month, Kislyak told The Post that he had also had communications with Flynn before the election.
