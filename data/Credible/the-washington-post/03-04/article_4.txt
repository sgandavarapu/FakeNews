data-hi-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/news/acts-of-faith/wp-content/uploads/sites/45/2017/03/tony.jpg&amp;w=1484\" data-low-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/news/acts-of-faith/wp-content/uploads/sites/45/2017/03/tony.jpg&amp;w=480\" class=\"pb-caption\">Tony Reali is shown with ashes on his forehead. (Courtesy of Tony Reali)</span> </div> <p>I guess I\u2019m giving up silence for Lent this year.</p> <p>That\u2019s an odd thing to say when you make a living yapping about sports on ESPN. And odder when the show I host, \u201cAround the Horn,\u201d makes a game out of LOUD NOISES. (That is to say, loud, perspicacious noises from the most insightful sportswriters in America!) I press a mute button to shut them down if (when!) our sports debate careens out of bounds (Fake News!). Silence is how I penalize. Silence works. But is silence good?</p> <p>I\u2019ve been on national television for 16 years and for all 16 I wore an ash on Ash Wednesday. I am grateful to ESPN and fortunate to work in an environment that allows me to be myself. But it\u2019s shocking to me that I\u2019m one of the few faces you see on TV wearing an ash. I did an interview where the reporter told me if you put \u201cThe Guy Who Wears Ashes on TV\u201d into Google, I\u2019m the first name that comes up. That\u2019s surprising. (Also true: I\u2019m the first name that comes up for \u201cThe Guy Who Proposed In Between The Men\u2019s and Women\u2019s Bathroom at LaGuardia Airport.\u201d Not as surprising.)</p> <p>Among ESPN viewers, it\u2019s well known I\u2019ll be wearing the ash and there\u2019s an expectation. I\u2019m not sure how I feel about that. Sports websites have kept a running tally of my ash through the years. \u2018How Heavy Thumbed Will Fr. Mario Be This Season?!\u2019 I see the humor in that; I was tattooed in \u201806, crop dusted in \u201809, this year: \u201cclean, minimal, very on trend for 2017,\u201d according to one tweeter. While I get questions like: \u201cdad n em wana kno wats on ur coconut man,\u201d the overwhelming energy has always been support and praise.</p> <p>I struggle with the publicness of the ash. I was rolling in the pew laughing (RIPL!) when this year\u2019s Gospel started with \u201cBeware of practicing your piety before others in order to be seen by them.\u201d Isn\u2019t that what I\u2019m doing when I get the ash in the afternoon and go on TV a few hours later? Could I not go to Mass after work? I\u2019m still not sure I have answers to those questions. I want viewers to see an authentic version of me, and on this one day that includes the ash.</p> <p>Which brings me back to silence. A thought at this moment: I do it publicly one day, think about those who are questioned about their faith every day. Those who are demanded to answer for their faith every day. Those who are threatened because of their faith every day. Or more to the point, think of those whose beliefs and life are prejudged and silenced every day because their ash is a hijab or turban or yarmulke or a passport that has a foreign birth country.</p> <p>If the Christian Brothers and Jesuits taught me anything, it\u2019s that you can\u2019t have apprehension without challenging your beliefs. Educate, question, challenge, re-educate, re-question, re-challenge. If that\u2019s true for one\u2019s personal faith, isn\u2019t it also true of capital F, Faith? Isn\u2019t that what Pope Francis is doing right now? He\u2019s opened a window and let fresh air into a room.</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-photo-right\" style=\"width:208px;\"> <a name=\"f9e1d1f002\"></a> <img alt=\"Tony Reali, 8, First Communion (Photo courtesy of Tony Reali)\" src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/news/acts-of-faith/wp-content/uploads/sites/45/2017/03/IMG_1139-208x300.jpg\"/> <span class=\"pb-caption\">Tony Reali, 8, at his First Holy Communion (Courtesy of Tony Reali)</span> </div> <p>My church experience began pretty conventionally and fitting for someone named Anthony Joseph Paul Reali. (Can\u2019t you just see the white suit at First Holy Communion?) Church every Sunday. Knights of Columbus free-throw-shooting contests. There was St. Charles in Staten Island, St. Gabriel\u2019s in New Jersey, Christian Brothers Academy and Fordham University. Growing up in the church in the \u201880s meant a lot of \u201cHere I Am, Lord.\u201d</p> <p>When I was based in Washington, D.C., for \u201cPardon the Interruption\u201d and \u201cAround the Horn,\u201d I became a parishioner at a church that changed me forever: St. Augustine\u2019s in Northwest D.C. \u201cThe Mother Church of Black Catholics\u201d was a jaw-dropping experience for me. A spirituality vortex! The music. The homilies.</p> <p>Fr. Pat and Fr. Paul spent Sunday after Sunday talking about being Catholic today, right now, this second. They talked about being a Catholic whose feet were on the street \u2014 \u201cFeet On The Street\u201d \u2014 being the community outreach group to homeless people. They talked about Trayvon Martin and whatever else was in the news. I was in a church that was so current, a parish that put its heart outside of its body for all to see. I can\u2019t imagine there\u2019s a parish anywhere with more voice.</p> <p>Realizing faith and spirituality can have that type of current voice is a powerful thing when you consider where we are in the world today. The Catholic Church is no different from\u00a0any other world religion in the constant need for fresh air. I\u2019d consider it a necessary challenge for all religions and value systems: How do you re-evaluate and remain open-minded as the world thankfully progresses? What happens if it feels like the world isn\u2019t progressing the way it should?</p> <p>Trying to answer that last question is like hugging smoke. But I think I know how I want my answer to start: by not being silent. Change starts with voices. Those voices might be sparked by anger \u2014 maybe we have to allow for that \u2014 but they can\u2019t only be anger. What if the voice comes from empathy? What if the way to move forward is by staying engaged with other people and putting our feet on the street? What if we saw people as the human beings they are, not the scarf on their head, country on the passport, or ash on their face? These are the questions I find I\u2019m asking myself. Questions that should not be silenced.</p> <p><em>Tony Reali is the host of the ESPN program, \u201cAround the Horn.\u201d It airs weeknights at 5 p.m. (EST).</em></p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0.5625\" data-category-id=\"wirereuters\" data-duration=\"83400\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"Pope Francis leads Ash Wednesday mass in Rome\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" data-max-width=\"-1\" data-object-id=\"58b726cbe4b059a9e421f8e0\" data-show-caption=\"1\" data-show-endscreen=\"1\" data-show-posttv-overlay=\"1\" data-show-promo=\"http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/03-04-2017/t_1488647116600_name_20170304_ASHWEDNESDAY_REUTERS.jpg\" data-uuid=\"cdde0c16-feb8-11e6-9b78-824ccab94435\" data-variants=\"0\" data-video-360=\"0\" data-youtube-id=\"DGXlfsocO5A\"> <script src=\"//js.washingtonpost.com/video/resources/env/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js\"></script> </div> <div class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\">Pope Francis joins a traditional procession and holds mass on Ash Wednesday to mark the start of Lent.
