night and was arrested on the South Lawn of the presidential residence, according to the U.S. Secret Service.</p> <p>The suspect \u2014 identified by D.C. police as Jonathan Tran, 26 \u2014 was being charged and could make an initial appearance in D.C. Superior Court later on Saturday on a charge of unlawful entry.</p> <p>A federal law enforcement official said the man arrested has no prior criminal record and had no previous history with the Secret Service. The official spoke on the condition of not being named to discuss background information about the suspect that is not usually made public by police. Tran is from the city of Milpitas, just outside of San Jose.</p> <p>On Saturday, President Trump said the \u201cSecret Service service did a fantastic job last night,\u201d and he described the suspect as a \u201ctroubled person.\u201d</p> <p>The U.S. Secret Service said the breach occurred at 11:38 p.m. when the person managed to get over an outer-perimeter fence near the Treasury Building on East Executive Avenue. Uniformed members of the Secret Service arrested him on the South Lawn.</p><div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0.5625\" data-category-id=\"wusa9\" data-duration=\"97271\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"White House fence could go up about five feet\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" data-max-width=\"-1\" data-object-id=\"572366d1e4b0bda369e39755\" data-show-caption=\"1\" data-show-endscreen=\"1\" data-show-posttv-overlay=\"1\" data-show-promo=\"http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/03-11-2017/t_1489252801155_name_20170311_whitehousesecurity_AP.jpg\" data-uuid=\"71b99934-0e11-11e6-bc53-db634ca94a2a\" data-variants=\"0\" data-video-360=\"0\" data-youtube-id=\"3T5sV5lJ4FY\"> <script src=\"//js.washingtonpost.com/video/resources/env/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js\"></script> </div> <div class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\"> (WUSA 9)</span> </div> </div> <p>Authorities said the person was carrying a backpack that was searched. It did not contain any hazardous materials, the Secret Service statement said.</p> <p>Further searches of the grounds turned up nothing suspicious, according to law enforcement authorities. Trump was in the White House at the time.</p> <p>Friday\u2019s night\u2019s fence jumper is believed to the first since Trump took office. In the previous two years, many people tried or succeeded in getting into the most secure residence in the world. Last year, the Secret Service added small spikes \u2014 or \u201cpencil points\u201d \u2014 to the top of the six-foot fence that surrounds the White House complex. The agency also announced a plan to raise the height of the security fence to 11 feet by 2018.</p> <p>Some break in to make political statements or are emotionally disturbed. One man last year jumped the fence while being chased by people after seeing him commit a robbery. In 2015, a Connecticut man scaled the fence while draped in an American flag on Thanksgiving Day. He had left a suicide note with his friends.</p> <p>In May, a Secret Service agent shot and critically wounded a man who approached a guard booth outside the White House and refused to put down the pistol he was carrying.</p> <p>Perhaps the most serious breach was on Sept. 19, 2014, when Omar Gonzalez climbed over the north fence and made his way deep into the White House. When he was finally tackled by an off-duty Secret Service agent in the ornate East Room, he was found to have a knife in a pants pocket. Two hatchets, a machete and 800 rounds of ammunition were found in his car nearby.
