(Victoria Walker/The Washington Post)</span> </div> </div> <p>Attorney General Jeff Sessions's spokeswoman released a statement late Wednesday after <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/sessions-spoke-twice-with-russian-ambassador-during-trumps-presidential-campaign-justice-officials-say/2017/03/01/77205eda-feac-11e6-99b4-9e613afeb09f_story.html?utm_term=.b251b38b017f\">The Washington Post reported Sessions had spoken twice with Russia's ambassador last year</a> \u2014 contacts he failed to disclose in his confirmation hearings.</p> <p>\u201cI never met with any Russian officials to discuss the issues of the campaign,\u201d Sessions said. \u201cI have no idea what this allegation is about. It is false.\u201d</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-html\"> <blockquote class=\"twitter-tweet\" data-width=\"500\"> <p dir=\"ltr\" lang=\"en\">Statement from Attorney General Sessions: <a href=\"https://t.co/kZic12DW0R\">pic.twitter.com/kZic12DW0R</a></p> <p>\u2014 Sarah Isgur Flores (@whignewtons) <a href=\"https://twitter.com/whignewtons/status/837159285792600066\">March 2, 2017</a></p></blockquote> <p><script async=\"\" charset=\"utf-8\" src=\"//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js\"></script></p> </div> <p>This is a remarkably misleading non-denial denial. Sessions, in the course of three sentences, manufactures a straw man, props him up and then knocks him down. He is denying a report that doesn't exist.</p> <p>The fact is The Post never reported that Sessions and the Russian ambassador discussed \u201cissues of the campaign.\u201d The report was that he <em>met</em> with Russian Ambassador Sergey Kislyak, period \u2014 apparently despite the Trump campaign's repeated denials of contact with Russian officials <em>and</em> despite Sessions's own apparent denials during his confirmation hearings.</p> <p>It's a statement that suggests Sessions is on his heels\u00a0and needs to obfuscate. But just how much trouble is he in?</p> <p>Democrats quickly accused him of lying to Congress, which would constitute perjury given he was under oath. And some are calling for him to resign.</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0.5625\" data-category-id=\"segments\" data-duration=\"135087\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"Sessions \u2018unable to comment' on Trump intelligence briefing reports\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" data-max-width=\"-1\" data-object-id=\"58757029e4b0d001d66b5c7b\" data-show-caption=\"1\" data-show-endscreen=\"1\" data-show-posttv-overlay=\"1\" data-show-promo=\"http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/01-10-2017/t_1484091355019_name_2017_01_10T155656Z_1166849082_RC1B6FE12FF0_RTRMADP_3_USA_CONGRESS_SESSIONS.jpg\" data-uuid=\"b91d3ec0-d78d-11e6-a0e6-d502d6751bc8\" data-variants=\"0\" data-video-360=\"0\" data-youtube-id=\"RiIdLAVQIyg\"> <script src=\"//js.washingtonpost.com/video/resources/env/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js\"></script> </div> <div class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\">Sen. Al Franken (D-Minn.) questioned attorney general nominee Sen. Jeff Sessions (R-Ala.) about news that intelligence officials briefed President-elect Trump on unconfirmed reports that Russia has compromising information on Trump. (Senate Judiciary Committee)</span> </div> </div> <p>A parsing of Sessions's statements in his confirmation hearings suggests the most charitable conclusion is that he misunderstood the question he was being asked about contact with Russia and offered too broad a denial.\u00a0The\u00a0more apparent conclusion is that Sessions's denials are falling apart.</p> <p>Here's the exchange with Sen. Al Franken (D-Minn.) (who said in a statement Wednesday night that Sessions's comments were \u201cat best, misleading\"):</p> <blockquote class=\"citation\"> <p class=\"loose\"><span class=\"hit\">FRANKEN</span>: Okay. CNN has just published a story, and I'm telling you this about a news story that's just been published. I'm not expecting you to know whether or not it's true or not. But CNN just published a story alleging that the intelligence community provided documents to the president-elect last week that included information that, quote, \u201cRussian operatives claimed to have compromising personal and financial information about Mr. Trump.\u201d These documents also allegedly say, quote, \u201cThere was a continuing exchange of information during the campaign between Trump's surrogate<span class=\"hit\">s</span> and intermediaries for the Russian government.\u201d</p> <p class=\"loose\">Now, again, I'm telling you this as it's coming out, so you know. But if it's true, it's obviously extremely serious and if there is any evidence that anyone affiliated with the Trump campaign communicated with the Russian government in the course of this campaign, what will you do?</p> <p class=\"loose\">SESSIONS: Senator Franken, I'm not aware of any of those activities. I have been called a surrogate\u00a0at a time or two in that campaign and I didn't have \u2014 did not have communications with the Russians, and I'm unable to comment on it.</p> </blockquote> <p>Sarah Isgur Flores, Session's spokeswoman, explained in a separate statement to The Post on Wednesday night: \u201cHe was asked during the hearing about communications between Russia and the Trump campaign \u2014 not about meetings he took as a senator and a member of the Armed Services Committee.\u201d</p> <p>That's not really true.</p> <p>Sessions was clearly asked whether there was anyone \u201caffiliated with the Trump campaign\u201d who communicated with the Russian government <em>during</em> the campaign. Sessions admitted that he fit this definition of being affiliated with the campaign\u00a0\u2014 as a surrogate (and, really, a top adviser as well) \u2014 and said he \u201cdid not have communications with the Russians.\u201d</p> <p>But while the question was in the context of the campaign, it was not about whether the campaign was actually discussed or whether that was the purpose of the meeting. And Sessions's denial doesn't carve out any exceptions for talking about non-campaign matters with the Russian government. At the very least, you would expect Sessions, a lawyer, to be more careful with his words here. But he doesn't even use the non-denial denial\u00a0like he did in his statement Wednesday night.</p> <p>Sessions was also asked about contact with the Russians in a questionnaire from Sen. Pat Leahy (D-Vt.).</p> <p>Here's the question to which Sessions replied simply, \u201cNo\u201d:</p> <blockquote class=\"citation\"><p>Several of the President-elect\u2019s nominees or senior advisers have Russian ties. Have you been in contact with anyone connected to any part of the Russian government about the 2016 election, either before or after Election Day?</p></blockquote> <p>This question is clearly and more directly about discussion of actual campaign matters. So Sessions's denial in this case is narrower \u2014 as his statement Wednesday night was.</p> <p>But then the problem is the fact that Sessions apparently now doesn't remember what he spoke with the Russian ambassador about.\u00a0\u201cThere\u2019s just not strong recollection of what was said,\u201d one Justice Department official told The Post. So in one case, you have a denial that the campaign was discussed, and now the line is apparently that he doesn't <em>remember</em> talking about specific things like the campaign. It's all very reminiscent of Michael Flynn's statements that he couldn't recall discussing sanctions with the Russian ambassador \u2014 even though he had.</p> <p>But even that is apparently not quite true. An anonymous Trump administration official told John Harwood on Wednesday night that the election was indeed discussed, but only in \u201csuperficial\u201d terms and it wasn't the \u201csubstance of their discussion.\u201d</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-html\"> <blockquote class=\"twitter-tweet\" data-width=\"500\"> <p dir=\"ltr\" lang=\"en\">Trump admin official on Sessions/Russian envoy: \"superficial comments about election-related news, not substance of their discussion\"</p> <p>\u2014 John Harwood (@JohnJHarwood) <a href=\"https://twitter.com/JohnJHarwood/status/837137235908657154\">March 2, 2017</a></p></blockquote> <p><script async=\"\" charset=\"utf-8\" src=\"//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js\"></script></p> </div> <p>That's a lot of backtracking and non-denials in one night. And it's a sign of just how much trouble Sessions appears to be in. Whether he's ever in legal trouble over this is one thing; for now, he's got a major, major political problem, and so does the Trump administration.</p> <div class=\"inline-content
