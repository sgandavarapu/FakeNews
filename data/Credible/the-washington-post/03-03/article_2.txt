administration and its contacts with Russian officials is a \u201cwitch hunt\u201d fueled\u00a0by \u201cfake news\u201d instigated by leading Democrats looking to distract attention from their election defeat and carried out by their lap dogs in the U.S. media.</p> <p>In other words, Moscow\u2019s reaction\u00a0pretty much <a href=\"https://twitter.com/realDonaldTrump/status/837491607171629057\" target=\"_blank\">mirrors</a> that of President <a href=\"https://twitter.com/realDonaldTrump/status/837492425283219458\" target=\"_blank\">Trump</a> after Attorney General Jeff Sessions <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/powerpost/top-gop-lawmaker-calls-on-sessions-to-recuse-himself-from-russia-investigation/2017/03/02/148c07ac-ff46-11e6-8ebe-6e0dbe4f2bca_story.html?utm_term=.1b9096907241\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">recused himself</a> from any investigation into alleged Russian interference in the presidential election. Sessions made the move after The Washington Post <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/sessions-spoke-twice-with-russian-ambassador-during-trumps-presidential-campaign-justice-officials-say/2017/03/01/77205eda-feac-11e6-99b4-9e613afeb09f_story.html?utm_term=.9ea820a4fdf6\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">revealed</a> that he twice met with Russian Ambassador <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/moscows-man-in-washington-is-at-the-center-of-the-political-moment/2017/03/02/7e624234-ff6b-11e6-99b4-9e613afeb09f_story.html\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">Sergey Kislyak</a> last year, while still serving as a senator, but did not disclose that during his Senate confirmation hearing in January. Sessions was an early backer of Trump\u2019s bid for the presidency and served as an adviser and surrogate for his campaign.</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/powerpost/top-gop-lawmaker-calls-on-sessions-to-recuse-himself-from-russia-investigation/2017/03/02/148c07ac-ff46-11e6-8ebe-6e0dbe4f2bca_story.html?hpid=hp_hp-banner-main_sessionsreax-banner-435pm%3Ahomepage%2Fstory&amp;utm_term=.938b650ae177\" target=\"_self\">Sessions will recuse himself from any probe related to presidential campaign</a>]</i> </p> <p>Russia\u2019s foreign minister, Sergei Lavrov, said Friday that\u00a0\u201call this is very much reminiscent of a witch hunt and the McCarthyism era, which we all thought was long gone.\u201d</p> <p>Kremlin spokesman Dmitry Peskov, commenting on reports that Trump\u2019s son-in-law met with Kislyak in December, backed Trump\u2019s use of the phrase\u00a0\u201cwitch hunt,\u201d saying,\u00a0\u201cWe have nothing to add to President Trump\u2019s exhaustive definition.\u201d</p><div contexts.</p> <p>In the United States, the suggestion that Sessions was not forthcoming at his Senate hearing was enough to force him to step aside from potential probes, regardless of what he and Kislyak discussed.</p> <p>Moscow\u2019s reaction, meanwhile, has deeper roots than the controversy over Trump\u2019s ties to Russia. When Russian officialdom speaks, its comments reflect 25 years of growing frustration with the United States.\u00a0</p> <p>Moscow has never admitted to interfering in the election, as the U.S. intelligence community accuses it of doing, and sees any and all questions about Trump\u2019s ties to Russia as symptoms of what it considers rampant Russophobia in the United States\u2019 political and military establishment. Two prominent daily newspapers, Moskovsky Komsomolets and Nezavisimaya Gazeta, have featured commentary citing anti-Russian hysteria in the United States as a primary driver of efforts to <a href=\"http://wapo.st/2m9Vj9T\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"wapo.st\">oust</a> national security adviser Mike Flynn, who resigned last month, and force Sessions to recuse himself.\u00a0</p> <p>In Washington, Trump\u2019s <a href=\"http://edition.cnn.com/2016/12/30/politics/trump-putin-very-smart-for-decision-to-withhold-sanctions/\" target=\"_self\">warm words</a> for Russian President Vladi\u00admir Putin, his half-joking\u00a0<a href=\"https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/video/2016/jul/27/donald-trump-russia-dnc-email-hack-video\" target=\"_self\">call</a> for Russia to hack into Hillary Clinton\u2019s emails and the revelation that Flynn discussed <a href=\"http://wapo.st/2m9TZnE\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"wapo.st\">U.S. sanctions against Russia</a> with Kislyak before Trump took office have raised concerns that something more sinister is going on. Trump and his administration, though, have resisted accepting the U.S. intelligence community\u2019s conclusion that Russia was attempting to help him win the election. Sessions, in an interview Thursday on Fox News, declined to acknowledge that Russia favored Trump over Clinton.\u00a0</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/us-counterintelligence-officials-are-examining-possible-ties-between-russia-and-trump-associates/2017/01/19/7e10f31c-debd-11e6-918c-99ede3c8cafa_story.html?tid=a_inl&amp;utm_term=.59cfcfec905f\" target=\"_self\">U.S. officials look at possible ties between Russia, Trump associates</a>]</i> </p> <p>\u00a0In Moscow, suggestions that Trump colluded with Russian officials on the election or that the Kremlin is blackmailing him into cozying up to Putin are regarded as hangovers from the Obama administration. Some media outlets have reported that U.S. intelligence officials think the ambassador is a top Russian spy, accusations that Peskov dismissed as\u00a0\u201cbaseless fake-news stories.\u201d Peskov, borrowing from Trump\u2019s broadsides against leaks, advised reporters to rely \u201conly on official statements by genuine officials.\u201d</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/moscows-man-in-washington-is-at-the-center-of-the-political-moment/2017/03/02/7e624234-ff6b-11e6-99b4-9e613afeb09f_story.html\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">Moscow\u2019s man in Washington is at the center of the political moment</a>]</i> </p> <p>Russian officialdom and the White House also converge in their view of the U.S. media.</p> <p>Maria Zakharova, spokeswoman for Russia\u2019s Foreign Ministry, characterized stories about Trump this way: \u201cIs this rock bottom?
