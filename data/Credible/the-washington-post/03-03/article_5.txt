Jewish centers and schools in recent months, nor do they think he was behind the <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/post-nation/wp/2017/02/27/philadelphia-police-investigate-reprehensible-toppling-of-100-headstones-at-jewish-cemetery/?utm_term=.ff7d3f381614\" Even after Thompson was taken into custody in St. Louis, Jewish groups and officials remained on edge about the threats that are still unsolved.</p> <p>\u201cThere\u00a0are many more JCC\u00a0bomb threats that have not been solved, and communities are hurting,\u201d Evan Bernstein, the New York regional director at the Anti-Defamation League, told reporters\u00a0Friday. \u201cWe hope all law enforcement will continue to be diligent.\u201d</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-html\"> <blockquote class=\"twitter-tweet\" data-width=\"500\"> <p dir=\"ltr\" lang=\"en\"><a href=\"https://twitter.com/hashtag/FBI?src=hash\">#FBI</a> Dir. &amp; sr. execs met w/ community leaders about threats to Jewish institutions &amp; to affirm FBI's commitment to ensure safe communities <a href=\"https://t.co/u07T94T4Jb\">pic.twitter.com/u07T94T4Jb</a></p> <p>\u2014 FBI (@FBI) <a href=\"https://twitter.com/FBI/status/837736430990311424\">March 3, 2017</a></p></blockquote> <p><script async=\"\" charset=\"utf-8\" src=\"//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js\"></script></p> </div> <p>There have been more than 100 threats, according to the ADL. The FBI on Friday called its ongoing investigation into the threats \u201ca top priority\u201d for the bureau.</p> <p>\u201cAgents and analysts across the country are working to identify and stop those responsible,\u201d the bureau said in a statement. \u201cThe FBI is committed to ensuring that people of all races and religions feel safe in their communities and places of worship.\u201d</p> <p>FBI Director James B. Comey met Friday morning with Jewish community leaders to discuss the threats. In a statement, the Jewish groups said they \u201cexpressed the deep gratitude of the entire community for the extraordinary effort that the FBI is applying to the ongoing investigation.\u201d</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i> [<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/post-nation/wp/2017/02/28/trump-questioned-who-is-really-behind-anti-semitic-threats-and-vandalism-official-says/?utm_term=.74914812d0e3\">Trump questions who is really behind anti-Semitic threats and vandalism</a>] </i> </p> <p>Federal agents arrested Thompson on Friday morning, according to\u00a0the U.S. Attorney\u2019s Office in the Southern District of New York. Thompson \u2014 whose Twitter page is full of rants about white people and President Trump \u2014 was charged with cyberstalking and is accused of\u00a0communicating at least eight threats to Jewish Community Centers, which an FBI complaint said were \u201cpart of a sustained campaign to harass and intimidate\u201d a woman with whom he\u00a0had been romantically involved.</p> <p>Thompson previously drew national attention when he was\u00a0<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/erik-wemple/wp/2016/02/03/how-the-media-dealt-with-the-intercepts-retracted-story-on-dylann-roofs-cousin/?utm_term=.7c1b34af7af2\" target=\"_blank\">fired a little more than a year ago from the Intercept</a>, an investigative journalism website, for fabricating quotes and misleading colleagues to cover his tracks. In <a href=\"https://theintercept.com/2016/02/02/a-note-to-readers/\" target=\"_blank\">an editor\u2019s note</a>, the publication said Thompson had engaged in \u201ca pattern of deception\u201d and wrote that he created fake email accounts to impersonate people.</p> <p>\u201cWe were horrified to learn this morning that Juan Thompson, a former employee of The Intercept, has been arrested in connection with bomb threats against the ADL and multiple Jewish Community Centers in addition to cyberstalking,\u201d Charlotte Greensit, the Intercept\u2019s managing editor, said in a statement Friday. \u201cThese actions are heinous and should be fully investigated and prosecuted.\u201d</p> <p>Thompson appeared in federal court Friday wearing handcuffs and leg chains along with a faded blue denim shirt and beige jeans. He was accompanied by a U.S. marshal and a public defender. His mother, along with several other family members and friends, sat in the galley.</p> <p>After the hearing, Thompson was remanded to federal custody. When approached by a reporter, his mother and other relatives declined comment.</p> <p>An FBI spokeswoman said Thompson is not believed to be responsible for all of the threats to Jewish Community Centers across the country. Thompson was arrested in St. Louis, not far from a Jewish cemetery in suburban University City, Mo., where 150 headstones were recently vandalized, but the spokeswoman said agents do not think he is responsible for that incident.</p> <p>Doron Krakow, chief executive of the Jewish Community Center Association of North America, said the group is \u201cgratified\u201d by Thompson\u2019s arrest, though they hope officials will find those responsible for the other threats.</p> <p>\u201cIt\u2019s a good thing they got this guy, but this indictment brings us no closer to identifying the source of the broader campaign aimed at Jewish centers, with around 100 threats still unaccounted for, not to mention the numerous incidents of vandalism,\u201d J.M. Berger,\u00a0a fellow with the International Centre for Counter-Terrorism at The Hague and an expert on extremism, wrote in an email Friday.</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-photo-right\" style=\"width:362px;\"> <a name=\"98b0f15e5e\"></a> <img alt=\"\" src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_960w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/03/01/Local-Politics/Images/Jewish_Cemetery_Damage_50216-fc334.jpg\"/> <span class=\"pb-caption\">A Jewish community center and day school in Davie, Fla., after people were evacuated due to a threat Monday. (Wilfredo Lee/AP)</span> </div> <p>According to the complaint, after Thompson\u2019s relationship with the woman ended, he began sending defamatory emails and faxes to her workplace, falsely reporting that she was involved in criminal activity and making threats to the Jewish centers in her name.</p> <p>Last year, speaking to the <a href=\"http://www.riverfronttimes.com/stlouis/juan-thompson-landed-a-dream-job-writing-about-st-louis-for-the-national-media-but-was-any-of-it-true/Content?oid=3060756&amp;showFullText=true\" target=\"_blank\">Riverfront Times</a>, an alt-weekly in St. Louis, Thompson disputed the Intercept\u2019s characterization of his firing and said he would \u201ccome through on this.\u201d The story described Thompson as a young, handsome journalist who is \u201cmaddeningly elusive\u201d and acted cagey when asked for evidence of claims about having cancer and being accepted to law school.</p> <p>The Intercept\u2019s account of Thompson\u2019s fabrications is\u00a0echoed in the criminal complaint filed Friday. The complaint says that after he and the woman he dated broke up in July 2016, Thompson began emailing and faxing her company defamatory messages about her.</p> <p>In one, for example, a person claiming to be a TV producer for a national news organization said the woman had been pulled over for drunk driving and was being sued for spreading a sexually transmitted disease. The woman also received a series of bizarre texts and emails related to Thompson from someone claiming to be his friend. One said \u2014\u00a0falsely \u2014\u00a0that Thompson had been the victim of a robbery and shooting, and was on life support.</p> <p>The woman obtained a protective order against Thompson in August 2016 and renewed it in October and December. Law enforcement confronted Thompson in November 2016, after someone from his IP address reported to the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children that the woman possessed child pornography. A New York police\u00a0detective eventually got in touch with Thompson \u2014\u00a0who claimed his email accounts had been hacked \u2014\u00a0and told him he should not attempt to contact the victim.</p> <p>Attempts to reach the woman Friday were not successful.</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-html\"> <blockquote class=\"twitter-tweet\" data-width=\"500\"> <p dir=\"ltr\" lang=\"en\">Bharara: Everyone deserves to be free from fear &amp; discrimination based on religion, race, or ethnicity; that is who we are as a nation</p> <p>\u2014 US Attorney SDNY (@SDNYnews) <a href=\"https://twitter.com/SDNYnews/status/837676634702491648\">March 3, 2017</a></p></blockquote> <p><script async=\"\" charset=\"utf-8\" src=\"//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js\"></script></p> </div> <p>In the months that followed, Thompson began making threats to Jewish Community Centers, sometimes in the woman\u2019s\u00a0name\u00a0and sometimes in his own, though he claimed the woman\u00a0was trying to frame him, according to authorities.</p> <p>\u201cPeople can leverage a climate of hate to have much more magnified effect than they otherwise could\u2019ve,\u201d\u00a0Andrew Rehfeld, president of the Jewish Federation of St. Louis, said in an interview Friday.</p> <p>Thompson seemed to be aware of the news coverage of threats. Using an apparently hacked email address, he sent a message to the Anti-Defamation League in February 2017 saying the woman was \u201cbehind the bomb threats against jews. She lives in nyc and is making more bomb threats tomorrow.\u201d</p> <p>The next day, the ADL received a call that said someone would detonate an explosive at the group\u2019s New York headquarters. Police searched and did not find a bomb.</p> <p>Similar threats were relayed in various ways toward Jewish centers in Dallas and San Diego, a Jewish school in Farmington Hills, Mich., as well as the Jewish History Museum in Manhattan. A Jewish school in Manhattan received two separate threats, one of which said Thompson wanted \u201cto create Jewish newtown tomorrow,\u201d a reference to the mass shooting of 20 children at a school in Newtown, Conn.</p> <p>Thompson claimed on Twitter that the woman\u00a0was stalking and harassing him, and that law enforcement had questioned\u00a0him unfairly because of her. He also <a href=\"https://twitter.com/JuanMThompson/status/836267885962145793\" target=\"_blank\">tweeted</a>\u00a0about the threats, seeming to condemn them. \u201cAnother week, another round of threats against Jewish ppl. In the middle of the day, you know who\u2019s at a JCC? Kids. KIDS.\u201d</p> <p>Oren\u00a0Segal, director of the ADL\u2019s Center on Extremism, said in a conference call with reporters Friday that while he could not speculate about what was in Thompson\u2019s mind,\u00a0\u201cthreatening Jewish institutions is an anti-Semitic act.\u201d</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-photo inline-photo-normal\"> <a name=\"8af9692169\"></a> <img class=\"unprocessed\" data-hi-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/news/post-nation/wp-content/uploads/sites/23/2017/03/Screen-Shot-2017-03-03-at-10.52.18-AM.png&amp;w=1484\" data-low-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/news/post-nation/wp-content/uploads/sites/23/2017/03/Screen-Shot-2017-03-03-at-10.52.18-AM.png&amp;w=480\" data-raw-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/news/post-nation/wp-content/uploads/sites/23/2017/03/Screen-Shot-2017-03-03-at-10.52.18-AM.png\" src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/news/post-nation/wp-content/uploads/sites/23/2017/03/Screen-Shot-2017-03-03-at-10.52.18-AM.png&amp;w=480\"/><br/> <span class=\"pb-caption\">(A screenshot of Thompson\u2019s tweet.)</span> </div> <p>The criminal complaint filed in federal court points to <a href=\"https://twitter.com/JuanMThompson\" target=\"_blank\">his Twitter page</a>, where he rails about an\u00a0ex-girlfriend he describes as\u00a0a \u201cnasty/racist #whitegirl.\u201d Thompson\u2019s\u00a0page also expresses disdain for Trump and white people generally. \u201cWhite folk are trash,\u201d he wrote in a tweet about the Oscars. He praised black women for their opposition to Trump. \u201cThe only person to call Trump a fascist in the #dncdebate is a black woman,\u201d he wrote. \u201cOf course.
