(Monica Akhtar/The Washington Post)</span> </div> </div> <p>FBI Director James B. Comey asked the Justice Department this weekend to issue a statement refuting President Trump\u2019s claim that President Barack Obama ordered a wiretap of Trump\u2019s phones before the election, according to U.S. officials, but the department did not do so.</p> <p>Comey made the request Saturday after Trump accused Obama on Twitter of having his \u201c \u2018wires tapped\u2019 in Trump Tower.\u201d\u00a0The White House expanded on Trump\u2019s comments Sunday with a call for a congressional probe of his allegations.</p> <p>The revelation, <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/2017/03/05/us/politics/trump-seeks-inquiry-into-allegations-that-obama-tapped-his-phones.html?hp&amp;action=click&amp;pgtype=Homepage&amp;clickSource=story-heading&amp;module=first-column-region&amp;region=top-news&amp;WT.nav=top-news\">first reported by the New York Times</a>, underscores the fraught nature of the FBI\u2019s high-profile investigation into Russian meddling in the 2016 election. A key question fueling that inquiry is whether Trump associates colluded with Russian officials to help Trump win.</p> <p>Neither Justice nor the FBI would comment Sunday.</p> <p>The development came as Trump\u2019s charge against Obama \u2014 leveled without any evidence \u2014 was being rebuffed both inside and outside of the executive branch. It drew\u00a0a blunt, on-the-record denial by a top intelligence official who served in the Obama administration.</p> <p>Speaking on NBC News on Sunday morning, former director of national intelligence James R. Clapper Jr. denied that a Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act (FISA) wiretap was authorized against Trump or the campaign during his tenure.</p> <p>\u201cThere was no such wiretap activity mounted against the president-elect at the time as a candidate or against his campaign,\u201d Clapper <a href=\"http://www.nbcnews.com/meet-the-press/video/clapper-i-can-deny-wiretap-of-trump-tower-890505795832\">said on \u201cMeet the Press,\u201d</a>\u00a0adding that he would \u201cabsolutely\u201d have been informed if the FBI had received a FISA warrant against either.</p> <p>\u201cI can deny it,\u201d Clapper said emphatically.</p> <p>In his claims early Saturday morning, the president tweeted that he \u201cjust found out\u201d that Obama had \u201cmy 'wires tapped' in Trump Tower\u201d before the election. Trump compared the alleged action to \u201cMcCarthyism.\u201d</p> <p>\u201cIs it legal for a sitting President to be \u2018wire tapping\u2019 a race for president prior to an election?\u201d Trump asked in another tweet. \u201cTurned down by court earlier. A NEW LOW!\u201d</p> <p>By Sunday morning, the White House doubled down\u00a0on Trump\u2019s explosive tweet storm\u00a0and called\u00a0for the congressional probe.</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i> [<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/post-politics/wp/2017/03/04/trump-accuses-obama-of-nixonwatergate-plot-to-wire-tap-trump-tower/?utm_term=.c18cc120f522\">Trump, citing no evidence, accuses Obama of \u2018Nixon/Watergate\u2019 plot to wiretap Trump Tower</a>] </i> </p> <p>Current and former government officials said such surveillance\u00a0would not have been approved by any senior Justice official in the Obama administration. And Trump\u2019s allegation raised hackles in the FBI leadership, implying as it did that the bureau may have acted illegally to wiretap a presidential candidate without probable cause that he was an \u201cagent of a foreign power,\u201d as the foreign intelligence surveillance law requires.</p> <p>\u201cThis is Nixon/Watergate,\u201d Trump tweeted Saturday.</p> <p>A spokesman for Obama countered several hours later that the former president never authorized a wiretap of\u00a0Trump or any other American citizen.\u00a0\u201cAny suggestion otherwise is simply false,\u201d the spokesman said.</p> <p>White House press secretary Sean Spicer on Sunday cited \u201creports\u201d of \u201cpotentially politically motivated investigations\u201d during the 2016 campaign, calling them \u201ctroubling.\u201d But none of the media reports cited by the White House provides evidence of a politically motivated surveillance effort against Trump.</p> <p>\u201cPresident Donald J. Trump is requesting that as part of their <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/russia-is-the-slow-burn-of-the-trump-administration-and-its-not-going-away/2017/03/04/2776ad6e-00f8-11e7-8ebe-6e0dbe4f2bca_story.html?utm_term=.14d89a42d3e6\">investigation into Russian activity</a>, the congressional intelligence committees exercise their oversight authority to determine whether executive branch investigative powers were abused in 2016,\u201d Spicer said.\u00a0\u201cNeither the White House nor the President will comment further until such oversight is conducted,\u201d the statement added.</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0.5625\" data-category-id=\"segments\" data-duration=\"158144\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"10 reactions to Trump\u2019s wiretapping allegations against Obama\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" data-max-width=\"-1\" data-object-id=\"58bc5699e4b059a9e422f5d8\" data-show-caption=\"1\" data-show-endscreen=\"1\" data-show-posttv-overlay=\"1\" data-show-promo=\"http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/03-05-2017/t_1488754287854_name_20170305_trump_thumbnail.jpg\" data-uuid=\"37acce42-01d0-11e7-9d14-9724d48f5666\" data-variants=\"0\" data-video-360=\"0\" data-youtube-id=\"Q0mT0Puh5_s\"> <script src=\"//js.washingtonpost.com/video/resources/env/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js\"></script> </div> <div class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\">Former director of national intelligence James R. Clapper Jr. on March 5 denied that President Trump's 2016 campaign was wiretapped while senators of both parties weighed in on the allegations. (Bastien Inzaurralde/The Washington Post)</span> </div> </div> <p>Congressional committees in both the House and the Senate are probing not just suspected Russian efforts to undermine the 2016 election but any contacts between Russian officials and the Trump campaign.</p> <p>Comey\u2019s request is sure to raise eyebrows in light of his actions last year in the bureau\u2019s investigation into Hillary Clinton\u2019s email server.</p> <p>In\u00a0July he held a news conference \u2014 without telling the Justice Department what he would say \u2014 to announce that the bureau had concluded Clinton did not commit a prosecutable offense. Then, 11 days before the election, Comey wrote Congress despite warnings from senior Justice officials that doing so would violate department policy and said the FBI was examining new emails that had come to light. Nothing came of the bureau\u2019s additional review, but Comey took heat for his actions, which Democrats say influenced the outcome of the election.</p> <p>It is not clear why Comey, who is the senior-most law enforcement officer who has been overseeing the FBI investigation from its inception in the Obama administration, did not himself issue a statement to refute Trump\u2019s claims. Nor is it clear to whom he made his request. Attorney General Jeff Sessions recused himself last week from all investigative matters related to the Trump campaign and any potential Russia links. The acting deputy attorney general, Dana Boente, a career federal prosecutor in the Eastern District of Virginia, is now overseeing the probe.</p> <p>Trump\u2019s tweets early Saturday\u00a0may have been prompted by the comments of a <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/trump-accuses-obama-of-nixonwatergate-wiretap--but-offers-no-evidence/2017/03/04/1ddc35e6-0114-11e7-8ebe-6e0dbe4f2bca_story.html?hpid=hp_hp-top-table-main_trumpwiretap-8pm%3Ahomepage%2Fstory&amp;utm_term=.144df582712b\">conservative radio host,</a>\u00a0which were summarized in an article on the conservative website Breitbart. The Breitbart story <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/trump-accuses-obama-of-nixonwatergate-wiretap--but-offers-no-evidence/2017/03/04/1ddc35e6-0114-11e7-8ebe-6e0dbe4f2bca_story.html?hpid=hp_hp-top-table-main_trumpwiretap-8pm%3Ahomepage%2Fstory&amp;utm_term=.144df582712b\">circulated among Trump\u2019s senior aides</a>\u00a0on Friday.</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i> [<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/just-how-would-the-government-obtain-a-wiretap-in-a-counterintelligence-probe-show-a-federal-judge-probable-cause-that-the-target-is-an-agent-of-a-foreign-power/2017/03/04/5905d7b4-0117-11e7-99b4-9e613afeb09f_story.html?utm_term=.079acc2bc36c\">How hard is it to get an intelligence wiretap? Pretty hard.</a>] </i> </p> <p>The White House\u2019s escalation of Trump\u2019s claims were kept at arm\u2019s length by congressional Republicans appearing on Sunday morning news broadcasts.</p> <p>When asked about Trump\u2019s allegations, Senate Intelligence Committee member Tom Cotton (R-Ark.) declined to comment on the tweets but said he has \u201cseen no evidence of the allegations.\u201d</p> <p>\u201cWhether that\u2019s a FISA court application or denial of that application or a re-submission of that application, that doesn\u2019t mean that none of these things happened. It just means we haven\u2019t seen that yet,\u201d Cotton added, speaking on \u201cFox News Sunday.\u201d</p> <p>Sen. Marco Rubio (R-Fla.) said\u00a0he is not aware of evidence to back up the president\u2019s claim. \u201cI have no insight into exactly what he\u2019s referring to,\u201d he\u00a0<a href=\"http://www.nbcnews.com/meet-the-press/video/full-rubio-interview-i-have-no-insight-into-trump-wiretap-tweets-890495555545\">said on \u201cMeet the Press.\u201d </a>\u201cThe president put that out there, and now the White House will have to answer for exactly what he was referring to.\u201d</p> <p>Obama\u2019s allies were more blunt, denying flatly that the former president had ordered a wiretap of\u00a0Trump\u2019s campaign.</p> <p>\u201cThis may come as a surprise to the current occupant of the Oval Office, but the president of the United States does not have the authority to unilaterally order the wiretapping of American citizens,\u201d said former Obama White House press secretary Josh Earnest.<br/> <strong><br/> </strong>Senate Minority Leader Charles E. Schumer (D-N.Y.) told \u201cMeet the Press\u201d that Trump is \u201cin trouble\u201d and acting \u201cbeneath the dignity of the presidency.\u201d</p> <p>\u201cThe president\u2019s in trouble if he falsely spread this kind of information,\u201d Schumer said. \u201cIt shows this president doesn\u2019t know how to conduct himself.\u201d</p> <p>Earnest added that Trump was attempting to distract from the controversy involving contacts between his campaign aides, including now-Attorney General Jeff Sessions, and Russian officials.</p> <p>\u201cWe know exactly why president Trump tweeted what he tweeted,\u201d Earnest added. \u201cBecause there is one page in the Trump White House crisis management playbook, and that is simply to tweet or say something outrageous to distract from a scandal. And the bigger the scandal, the more outrageous the tweet.\u201d</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i> [<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-fix/wp/2017/03/02/the-one-big-thing-i-still-dont-get-about-jeff-sessionss-answers-on-russia/?utm_term=.27f98ec63809\">The one big question Jeff Sessions still hasn\u2019t answered</a>] </i> </p> <p>But appearing on ABCs \u201cThis Week,\u201d White House deputy press secretary Sarah Huckabee Sanders repeatedly said that the president\u2019s allegation was worth looking into.</p> <p>\u201cHe\u2019s asking that we get down to the bottom of this, let\u2019s get the truth here, let\u2019s find out,\u201d Huckabee Sanders said. \u201cI think the bigger story isn\u2019t who reported it, but is it true. And I think the American people have a right to know if this happened, because if it did, again, this is the largest abuse of power that, I think, we have ever seen.\u201d</p> <p>Asked whether Trump truly believes Obama wiretapped him, Huckabee Sanders deflected.</p> <p>\u201cI would say that his tweet speaks for itself there,\u201d she said.</p> <p>Clapper\u2019s comments referred only to whether Trump campaign officials had been wiretapped. But their conversations could also have been captured by routine surveillance of Russian diplomats or intelligence operatives.</p> <p>U.S. monitoring of Russian Ambassador Sergey Kislyak, for example, caught his conversations with Trump adviser Michael Flynn during the campaign. Flynn went on to become\u00a0Trump's national security adviser, but he was forced to resign last month after admitting that he had misled other senior Trump officials about the nature of those conversations.</p> <p>The FBI and the National Security Agency also have obtained intercepted communications among Russians officials in which they refer to conversations with members of the Trump team, current and former U.S. officials have said.</p> <p>On the broader question of apparent Russian interference in the 2016 election, Clapper urged congressional investigators to attempt to settle the issue, which he said has become a \u201cdistraction\u201d in the political sphere.</p> <p>The intelligence community found no evidence of collusion between the Trump campaign and the Russian government \u2014 at least until the end of the Obama administration, he said Sunday.</p> <p>\u201cWe had no evidence of such collusion,\u201d he said on \u201cMeet the Press.\u201d But Clapper\u00a0added a caveat: \u201cThis could have unfolded or become available in the time since I left government.\u201d</p> <p>Whether there was any collusion is a key question fueling a wide-ranging federal probe into Russian interference in the 2016 presidential campaign.</p> <p>On Jan. 6, the U.S. spy agencies collectively released a report concluding that Russia carried out cyberhacks and other \u201cactive measures\u201d with an intent to help Trump and harm the campaign and potential presidency of Hillary Clinton. The report, Clapper pointed out, included \u201cno evidence\u201d of collusion with the Trump campaign.</p> <p>But the investigation by the FBI, the NSA and the CIA continues. (Monica Akhtar/The Washington Post)\n\nFBI Director James B. Comey asked the Justice Department this weekend to issue a statement refuting President Trump\u2019s claim that President Barack Obama ordered a wiretap of Trump\u2019s phones before the election, according to U.S. officials, but the department did not do so.\n\nComey made the request Saturday after Trump accused Obama on Twitter of having his \u201c \u2018wires tapped\u2019 in Trump Tower.\u201d The White House expanded on Trump\u2019s comments Sunday with a call for a congressional probe of his allegations.\n\nThe revelation, first reported by the New York Times, underscores the fraught nature of the FBI\u2019s high-profile investigation into Russian meddling in the 2016 election. A key question fueling that inquiry is whether Trump associates colluded with Russian officials to help Trump win.\n\nNeither Justice nor the FBI would comment Sunday.\n\nThe development came as Trump\u2019s charge against Obama \u2014 leveled without any evidence \u2014 was being rebuffed both inside and outside of the executive branch. It drew a blunt, on-the-record denial by a top intelligence official who served in the Obama administration.\n\nSpeaking on NBC News on Sunday morning, former director of national intelligence James R. Clapper Jr. denied that a Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act (FISA) wiretap was authorized against Trump or the campaign during his tenure.\n\n\u201cThere was no such wiretap activity mounted against the president-elect at the time as a candidate or against his campaign,\u201d Clapper said on \u201cMeet the Press,\u201d adding that he would \u201cabsolutely\u201d have been informed if the FBI had received a FISA warrant against either.\n\n\u201cI can deny it,\u201d Clapper said emphatically.\n\nIn his claims early Saturday morning, the president tweeted that he \u201cjust found out\u201d that Obama had \u201cmy 'wires tapped' in Trump Tower\u201d before the election. Trump compared the alleged action to \u201cMcCarthyism.\u201d\n\n\u201cIs it legal for a sitting President to be \u2018wire tapping\u2019 a race for president prior to an election?\u201d Trump asked in another tweet. \u201cTurned down by court earlier. And Trump\u2019s allegation raised hackles in the FBI leadership, implying as it did that the bureau may have acted illegally to wiretap a presidential candidate without probable cause that he was an \u201cagent of a foreign power,\u201d as the foreign intelligence surveillance law requires.\n\n\u201cThis is Nixon/Watergate,\u201d Trump tweeted Saturday.\n\nA spokesman for Obama countered several hours later that the former president never authorized a wiretap of Trump or any other American citizen. \u201cAny suggestion otherwise is simply false,\u201d the spokesman said.\n\nWhite House press secretary Sean Spicer on Sunday cited \u201creports\u201d of \u201cpotentially politically motivated investigations\u201d during the 2016 campaign, calling them \u201ctroubling.\u201d But none of the media reports cited by the White House provides evidence of a politically motivated surveillance effort against Trump.\n\n\u201cPresident Donald J. Clapper Jr. on March 5 denied that President Trump's 2016 campaign was wiretapped while senators of both parties weighed in on the allegations. (Bastien Inzaurralde/The Washington Post)\n\nCongressional committees in both the House and the Senate are probing not just suspected Russian efforts to undermine the 2016 election but any contacts between Russian officials and the Trump campaign.\n\nComey\u2019s request is sure to raise eyebrows in light of his actions last year in the bureau\u2019s investigation into Hillary Clinton\u2019s email server.\n\nIn July he held a news conference \u2014 without telling the Justice Department what he would say \u2014 to announce that the bureau had concluded Clinton did not commit a prosecutable offense. Nothing came of the bureau\u2019s additional review, but Comey took heat for his actions, which Democrats say influenced the outcome of the election.\n\nIt is not clear why Comey, who is the senior-most law enforcement officer who has been overseeing the FBI investigation from its inception in the Obama administration, did not himself issue a statement to refute Trump\u2019s claims. Nor is it clear to whom he made his request. Attorney General Jeff Sessions recused himself last week from all investigative matters related to the Trump campaign and any potential Russia links. The acting deputy attorney general, Dana Boente, a career federal prosecutor in the Eastern District of Virginia, is now overseeing the probe.\n\nTrump\u2019s tweets early Saturday may have been prompted by the comments of a conservative radio host, which were summarized in an article on the conservative website Breitbart.
