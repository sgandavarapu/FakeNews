released legislation that would substantially alter the Affordable Care Act. (Bastien Inzaurralde,Victoria Walker,Sarah Parnass/The Washington Post)</span> </div> </div> <p>As they <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/powerpost/house-leaders-brace-for-the-task-ahead-selling-obamacare-lite/2017/03/07/ab2f721e-02e5-11e7-ad5b-d22680e18d10_story.html\" target=\"_blank\">roll out their Obamacare replacement plan</a>, Republicans are quickly finding out what Democrats learned eight years ago: Even if you win control of Congress and the White House, there are still plenty of obstacles to passing\u00a0laws that, in principle, your whole party agrees with.</p> <p>Several factions within the Republican Party don't like some key details\u00a0about this new health-care plan. In fact, there's enough opposition that\u00a0these Republicans could derail the bill as it stands. It's something President Trump appeared to acknowledge when he offered up in a tweet Tuesday\u00a0morning the opportunity\u00a0for \u201creview and negotiation.\u201d</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-html\"> <blockquote class=\"twitter-tweet\" data-width=\"500\"> <p dir=\"ltr\" lang=\"en\">Our wonderful new Healthcare Bill is now out for review and negotiation. ObamaCare is a complete and total disaster - is imploding fast!</p> <p>\u2014 Donald J. Trump (@realDonaldTrump) <a href=\"https://twitter.com/realDonaldTrump/status/839086723552411648\">March 7, 2017</a></p></blockquote> <p><script async=\"\" charset=\"utf-8\" src=\"//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js\"></script></p> </div> <p>Trump\u00a0appears to have done his math. Assuming\u00a0no Democrats in either chamber support the bill, Republicans can't lose more than two GOP senators or 21 Republicans in the House. That doesn't leave a lot of wiggle room.</p> <p>Which means the factions opposed to Obamacare suddenly have a whole lot of leverage. Here's a look at the three main ones:</p> <h3>1) Western/Midwestern GOP senators: Don\u2019t touch Medicaid in our states</h3> <div class=\"inline-content inline-photo inline-photo-normal\"> <a name=\"2f31e897ac\"></a> <img class=\"unprocessed\" data-hi-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_960w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2016/11/06/National-Politics/Images/617990882.jpg&amp;w=1484\" data-low-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_960w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2016/11/06/National-Politics/Images/617990882.jpg&amp;w=480\" data-raw-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_960w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2016/11/06/National-Politics/Images/617990882.jpg\" src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_960w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2016/11/06/National-Politics/Images/617990882.jpg&amp;w=480\"/><br/> <span class=\"pb-caption\">Sen. Rob Portman (R-Ohio). (Spencer Platt/Getty Images)</span> </div> <p><strong>What they're saying</strong>: \u201cWe will not support a plan that does not include stability for Medicaid expansion populations or flexibility for states.\u201d</p> <p><strong>Who's saying it</strong>: Sens. Rob Portman (Ohio), Shelley Moore Capito (W.Va.), Cory Gardner (Colo.) and Lisa Murkowski (Alaska) wrote this in a letter to Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell (R-Ky.), <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/powerpost/house-leaders-brace-for-the-task-ahead-selling-obamacare-lite/2017/03/07/ab2f721e-02e5-11e7-ad5b-d22680e18d10_story.html\" target=\"_blank\">reported WaPo's\u00a0congressional team</a>.</p> <p>This faction could soon grow, says Molly Reynolds, a congressional analyst with the Brookings Institution. GOP senators in Nevada and Arizona, two states that adopted the Medicaid expansion, are up for reelection in 2018 and may be under pressure back home to protect millions of people from losing their Medicaid coverage \u2014 or the state having to bankroll those people. A significant number of Republican governors also want to keep the Medicaid expansion in place.</p> <p><b>Their argument, explained</b>: One of Obamacare's\u00a0primary\u00a0tools to ensure that low-income Americans received coverage was to expand Medicaid to people above the poverty line, an expansion the federal government mostly paid for. (Over the years, Obamacare would wean states off federal funding.) Thirty-one states agreed to the plan.</p> <p>These senators are saying that if you whittle\u00a0down\u00a0how much the federal government funds an expanded Medicaid program (as this plan does), you will leave states on the hook financially to cover all those people. Or those people will simply watch their coverage lapse.</p> <p>That's a lot of money. In fact, a January report by the nonpartisan Urban Institute found that Medicaid is the largest component of state spending in fiscal 2016 \u2014 almost a third of the budget.</p> <h3>2) Conservative/libertarian senators: Don\u2019t subsidize people's health care</h3> <div class=\"inline-content inline-html\"> <blockquote class=\"twitter-tweet\" data-width=\"500\"> <p dir=\"ltr\" lang=\"en\">Th House leadership plan is Obamacare Lite. It will not pass. Conservarives are not going to take it. <a href=\"https://twitter.com/hashtag/FullRepeal?src=hash\">#FullRepeal</a></p> <p>\u2014 Senator Rand Paul (@RandPaul) <a href=\"https://twitter.com/RandPaul/status/839105959918190592\">March 7, 2017</a></p></blockquote> <p><script async=\"\" charset=\"utf-8\" src=\"//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js\"></script></p> </div> <p><strong>Who's saying it</strong>: Sen. Rand Paul (Ky.) -- yes, the guy who <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-fix/wp/2017/03/02/rand-paul-a-copy-machine-and-a-secret-obamacare-bill/\" target=\"_blank\">led that quixotic crusade with a copy machine</a> to try to find the \u201csecret\u201d Obamacare bill the other day \u2014 is one of the most vocal.</p> <p>But there are also plenty of House Freedom Caucus members, that ultraconservative group of lawmakers who generally oppose increasing federal spending, joining him.</p> <p>These lawmakers are buoyed <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/powerpost/conservative-groups-and-lawmakers-demanding-full-repeal-could-derail-obamacare-rollback/2017/03/02/0bf3f1a0-feaa-11e6-8ebe-6e0dbe4f2bca_story.html\" target=\"_blank\">by several influential conservative groups</a> on the right that plan to oppose any bill they deem is not a \u201cfull repeal\" -- i.e. no tax credits. In a press release Tuesday, the conservative Club For Growth dubbed\u00a0this bill \"RyanCare\" -- and they didn't mean it as a compliment.</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-photo inline-photo-normal\"> <a name=\"4f118e7b2d\"></a> <img class=\"unprocessed\" data-hi-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/blogs/the-fix/files/2017/03/ClubForGrowth.png&amp;w=1484\" data-low-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/blogs/the-fix/files/2017/03/ClubForGrowth.png&amp;w=480\" data-raw-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/blogs/the-fix/files/2017/03/ClubForGrowth.png\" src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/blogs/the-fix/files/2017/03/ClubForGrowth.png&amp;w=480\"/><br/> </div><p></p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0.5625\" data-category-id=\"segments\" data-duration=\"49941\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"Rand Paul on House GOP health-care proposal: 'Sounds a lot like Obamacare Lite'\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" data-max-width=\"-1\" data-object-id=\"58bef144e4b059a9e423945d\" data-show-caption=\"1\" data-show-endscreen=\"1\" data-show-posttv-overlay=\"1\" data-show-promo=\"http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/03-07-2017/t_1488908374996_name_20170307_randpaul_thumb.jpg\" data-uuid=\"98853a04-035d-11e7-9d14-9724d48f5666\" data-variants=\"0\" data-video-360=\"0\" data-youtube-id=\"Bg2bFJ17O-c\"> <script src=\"//js.washingtonpost.com/video/resources/env/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js\"></script> </div> <div class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\">A day after House Republicans released a plan to supplant the Affordable Care Act, Sen. Rand Paul (R-Ky.) outlined the issues he has with their proposal, indicating it might be unconstitutional. (Alice Li,Jayne Orenstein/The Washington Post)</span> </div> </div> <p><strong>What they mean</strong>: The government shouldn\u2019t be funding people\u2019s health care for two reasons: 1) That's what Obamacare did. 2) It could end up costing the government more money in the long run, and that's a non-starter for many of these budget hawks, especially given that Trump's wish list on infrastructure and a U.S.-Mexico border wall could get real expensive, real quick.</p> <p>\u201cThe fundamental question is, when we see the full plan, will it reduce health-care costs and will it do so over time?\u201d <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/powerpost/house-leaders-brace-for-the-task-ahead-selling-obamacare-lite/2017/03/07/ab2f721e-02e5-11e7-ad5b-d22680e18d10_story.html\" target=\"_blank\">said</a> Rep. Mark Meadows (N.C.), chairman of the House Freedom Caucus. \u201cAnd when will we see the repeal of Obamacare insurance mandates if this is enacted?\u201d</p> <h3>3) The moderate Planned Parenthood supporters</h3> <div class=\"inline-content inline-photo inline-photo-normal\"> <a name=\"93767bd083\"></a> <img class=\"unprocessed\" data-hi-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_960w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2016/06/06/National-Politics/Images/Campaign_2016_The_Latest-4179c.jpg&amp;w=1484\" data-low-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_960w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2016/06/06/National-Politics/Images/Campaign_2016_The_Latest-4179c.jpg&amp;w=480\" data-raw-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_960w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2016/06/06/National-Politics/Images/Campaign_2016_The_Latest-4179c.jpg\" src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_960w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2016/06/06/National-Politics/Images/Campaign_2016_The_Latest-4179c.jpg&amp;w=480\"/><br/> <span class=\"pb-caption\">Sen. Susan Collins (R-Maine). (J. Scott Applewhite/Associated Press)</span> </div> <p><strong>What they're saying</strong>: Well, nothing yet. But this bill does cut off most federal funding for the nonprofit women's health-care clinic, which performs abortions and has been in the crosshairs of conservatives for years.</p> <p>It's possible, Reynolds says, that moderate GOP senators oppose the bill\u00a0over this.</p> <p>Sen. Susan Collins (Maine) <a href=\"http://thehill.com/blogs/floor-action/senate/312907-gop-senator-dont-link-planned-parenthood-to-obamacare-repeal\" target=\"_blank\">said</a> in January that she was \u201cnot happy\u201d about the prospect of tying Planned Parenthood and Obamacare together, but she stopped short of saying that would prevent her from supporting the GOP bill. In 2015, she teamed up with Murkowski to try to keep defunding language out of an earlier Obamacare repeal bill.</p> <p>Collins <a href=\"http://thehill.com/blogs/floor-action/senate/312907-gop-senator-dont-link-planned-parenthood-to-obamacare-repeal\" target=\"_blank\">told reporters</a> in January that she thinks a \u201csignificant number\u201d of House Republicans, such as Rep. Charlie Dent (Pa.), might join her.</p> <p><strong>The bottom line:</strong>\u00a0We count six GOP senators with concerns about this bill and\u00a0an unclear number of House Republicans \u2014 somewhere in the range of a dozen. In the Senate, six is four too many. And that probably means\u00a0that the bill, as it was rolled out Tuesday, will have to change if Republicans want to get it to Trump's desk anytime soon.</p></article> <div class=\"clear\"></div>
