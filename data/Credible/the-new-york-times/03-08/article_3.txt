Eisenhower</a> that they did not speak during a frosty ride to the 1953 inauguration. <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/topic/person/ronald-reagan\">Ronald Reagan</a> publicly blamed his woes on the mess he said <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/topic/person/jimmy-carter\">Jimmy Carter</a> had left him, just as Mr. Obama from time to time <a href=\"http://www.nytimes.com/2009/06/12/us/politics/12memo.html\">pointed the finger</a> at <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/topic/person/george-w-bush\">George W. Bush</a>.</p><div id=\"story-ad--aggro-6\" class=\"story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent  ad-aggro-6 aggro-only\">\n    <div class=\"accessibility-ad-header visually-hidden\">\n    <p>Advertisement</p>\n</div>\n<a class=\"visually-hidden skip-to-text-link\" href=\"#story-continues-5\">Continue reading the main story</a>\n</div>\n<p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"433\" data-total-count=\"4232\" id=\"story-continues-5\">But none of those moments compared to what America has seen in recent days. \u201cTrump is on new ground in going after Obama,\u201d said the historian Robert Dallek, who has written acclaimed books on <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/topic/person/john-fitzgerald-kennedy\">John F. Kennedy</a>, <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/topic/person/lyndon-b-johnson\">Lyndon B. Johnson</a> and <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/topic/person/richard-milhous-nixon\">Richard M. Nixon</a>. Most presidents have publicly ignored their predecessors \u201cuntil we get to Trump,\u201d he added. \u201cHe is either ignorant of recent presidential history or simply doesn\u2019t care.\u201d</p><p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"446\" data-total-count=\"4678\">The closest analogue in modern times may have been Johnson and Nixon, both presidents who favored secret wiretapping. In his last days in office, Johnson was furious at Nixon after wiretaps revealed that a Republican intermediary seemed to be trying to <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/2017/01/02/us/politics/nixon-tried-to-spoil-johnsons-vietnam-peace-talks-in-68-notes-show.html?_r=0\">undercut possible peace talks</a> before the 1968 election. For his part, Nixon was convinced that Johnson had bugged him. Yet neither Johnson nor Nixon publicly aired those grievances at the time.</p><div id=\"story-ad--aggro-8\" class=\"story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent  ad-aggro-4 ad-aggro-8 aggro-only\">\n    <div class=\"accessibility-ad-header visually-hidden\">\n    <p>Advertisement</p>\n</div>\n<a class=\"visually-hidden skip-to-text-link\" href=\"#story-continues-6\">Continue reading the main story</a>\n</div>\n<p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"451\" data-total-count=\"5129\" id=\"story-continues-6\">\u201cThe Nixon tapes show that Nixon always thought that Johnson taped his 1968 campaign, and possibly Nixon himself,\u201d said Luke A. Nichter, a leading scholar of Nixon\u2019s secret Oval Office tapes at Texas A&amp;M University. \u201cNixon said that it was J. Edgar Hoover who told him this. However, based on the available records, the closest to wiretapping Nixon that L.B.J. ever came was monitoring the phone calls out of <a href=\"http://topics.nytimes.com/top/reference/timestopics/people/a/spiro_t_agnew/index.html\">Spiro Agnew</a>\u2019s campaign plane.\u201d</p><div id=\"story-ad-2\" class=\"story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent \">\n    <div class=\"accessibility-ad-header visually-hidden\">\n    <p>Advertisement</p>\n</div>\n<a class=\"visually-hidden skip-to-text-link\" href=\"#story-continues-7\">Continue reading the main story</a>\n</div>\n<p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"396\" data-total-count=\"5525\" id=\"story-continues-7\">Before last year\u2019s campaign, Mr. Obama told advisers that he was inclined to keep quiet after leaving office to give his successor a chance to govern, much as Mr. Bush did for him. But he expected that successor to be Hillary Clinton or even Jeb Bush or Marco Rubio. Mr. Trump was a different story, and Mr. Obama concluded he would speak out if he felt the nation\u2019s ideals were under threat.</p><div id=\"story-ad--aggro-10\" class=\"story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent  ad-aggro-10 aggro-only\">\n    <div class=\"accessibility-ad-header visually-hidden\">\n    <p>Advertisement</p>\n</div>\n<a class=\"visually-hidden skip-to-text-link\" href=\"#story-continues-8\">Continue reading the main story</a>\n</div>\n<p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"368\" data-total-count=\"5893\" id=\"story-continues-8\">Ten days into the new administration, when Mr. Trump issued his first temporary travel ban on visitors from seven largely Muslim countries and cited Mr. Obama\u2019s own actions as precedent, the former president did just that in a statement saying he \u201cfundamentally disagrees with the notion of discriminating against individuals because of their faith or religion.\u201d</p><p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"371\" data-total-count=\"6264\">His team did not wait even that long. The day after the inauguration, former Obama administration officials, including John Kerry, whose tenure as secretary of state had just ended, joined a women\u2019s march in Washington protesting Mr. Trump. Other officials appeared on television talk shows and newspaper op-ed pages to speak out against the new president\u2019s policies.</p><div id=\"story-ad--aggro-12\" class=\"story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent  ad-aggro-4 ad-aggro-6 aggro-only\">\n    <div class=\"accessibility-ad-header visually-hidden\">\n    <p>Advertisement</p>\n</div>\n<a class=\"visually-hidden skip-to-text-link\" href=\"#story-continues-9\">Continue reading the main story</a>\n</div>\n<p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"437\" data-total-count=\"6701\" id=\"story-continues-9\">Mr. Trump\u2019s team has been angered by the criticism but even more by what they see as the enemy within. With so few of his own political appointees in place, much of the government is still operating with acting officials, some held over from the Obama administration. Moreover, the federal Civil Service, while officially neutral politically, is not dominated by Trump supporters, judging by vote results in Washington and its suburbs.</p><p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"609\" data-total-count=\"7310\">So when Mark Levin, the conservative radio host, contended that Mr. Obama had targeted Mr. Trump for surveillance in what he called a \u201csilent coup,\u201d an assertion picked up by Breitbart News, the former website of the White House chief strategist Stephen K. Bannon, it struck a chord. Along with reports that in Mr. Obama\u2019s last days in office his administration changed the rules on distributing intelligence and made a point of spreading information about Mr. Trump\u2019s team and Russia to different parts of the government to \u201cpreserve\u201d it, the wiretapping allegation pushed Mr. Trump over the top.</p><p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"155\" data-total-count=\"7465\">\u201cIt\u2019s a sign of how deeply frustrated he is,\u201d Mr. Gingrich said. \u201cThey have a much bigger assault against them than people have had in the past.\u201d</p><p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"229\" data-total-count=\"7694\">And so, Mr. Gingrich added, Mr. Trump needs to figure out how to get control of his own bureaucracy. \u201cHe\u2019s not going to survive,\u201d he said, \u201cunless he profoundly rethinks what they\u2019re doing and how they\u2019re doing it.\u201d</p><footer
