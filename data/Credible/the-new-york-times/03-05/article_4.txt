The implication was clear: Strapping two of the engines together at the base of a missile was the secret to building an ICBM that could ultimately hurl warheads at the United States.</p><p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"177\" data-total-count=\"12909\">In September, he celebrated the <a href=\"http://www.nti.org/analysis/articles/north-koreas-nuclear-year-reviewand-whats-next/\">most successful test yet</a> of a North Korean nuclear weapon \u2014 one that exploded with more than twice the destructive force of the Hiroshima bomb.</p><div id=\"story-ad--aggro-32\" class=\"story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent  ad-aggro-4 ad-aggro-8 aggro-only\">\n    <div class=\"accessibility-ad-header visually-hidden\">\n    <p>Advertisement</p>\n</div>\n<a class=\"visually-hidden skip-to-text-link\" href=\"#story-continues-21\">Continue reading the main story</a>\n</div>\n<p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"275\" data-total-count=\"13184\" id=\"story-continues-21\">His next goal, experts say, is to combine those two technologies, shrinking his nuclear warheads to a size that can fit on an intercontinental missile. Only then can he credibly claim that his isolated country has the know-how to hit an American city thousands of miles away.</p><p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"273\" data-total-count=\"13457\">In the last year of his presidency, Mr. Obama often noted publicly that the North was learning from every nuclear and missile test \u2014 even the failures \u2014 and getting closer to its goal. In private, aides noticed he was increasingly disturbed by North Korea\u2019s progress.</p><div id=\"story-ad-5\" class=\"story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent \">\n    <div class=\"accessibility-ad-header visually-hidden\">\n    <p>Advertisement</p>\n</div>\n<a class=\"visually-hidden skip-to-text-link\" href=\"#story-continues-22\">Continue reading the main story</a>\n</div>\n<p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"496\" data-total-count=\"13953\" id=\"story-continues-22\">With only a few months left in office, he pushed aides for new approaches. At one meeting, he declared that he would have targeted the North Korean leadership and weapons sites if he thought it would work. But it was, as Mr. Obama and his assembled aides knew, an empty threat: Getting timely intelligence on the location of North Korea\u2019s leaders or their weapons at any moment would be almost impossible, and the risks of missing were tremendous, including renewed war on the Korean Peninsula.</p><h4 class=\"story-subheading story-content\" data-para-count=\"24\" data-total-count=\"13977\">Hard Decisions for Trump</h4>        <a class=\"visually-hidden skip-to-text-link\" href=\"#story-continues-23\">Continue reading the main story</a>\n    </div><!-- close story-body -->\n    <div class=\"supplemental \" id=\"supplemental-4\">\n    </div><!-- close supplemental -->\n</div><!-- close story-body-supplemental -->\n<div class=\"story-interrupter\" id=\"story-continues-23\">\n    <figure id=\"media-100000004870795\" class=\"media photo embedded layout-jumbo-horizontal media-100000004870795 ratio-tall\" data-media-action=\"modal\" itemprop=\"associatedMedia\" itemscope itemid=\"https://static01.nyt.com/images/2017/01/14/science/14MISSILE5/14MISSILE5-superJumbo.jpg\" itemtype=\"http://schema.org/ImageObject\" aria-label=\"media\" role=\"group\">\n    <span class=\"visually-hidden\">Photo</span>\n    <div class=\"image\">\n            <img src=\"https://static01.nyt.com/images/2017/01/14/science/14MISSILE5/14MISSILE5-superJumbo.jpg\" alt=\"\" class=\"media-viewer-candidate\" data-mediaviewer-src=\"https://static01.nyt.com/images/2017/01/14/science/14MISSILE5/14MISSILE5-superJumbo.jpg\" data-mediaviewer-caption=\"The single successful test flight in a run of Musudan missile failures came in June, shown in this image from North Korea&rsquo;s state-run news agency. Now, one of the immediate questions he faces is whether to accelerate or scale back those efforts.</p><div id=\"story-ad--aggro-36\" class=\"story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent  ad-aggro-4 ad-aggro-6 aggro-only\">\n    <div class=\"accessibility-ad-header visually-hidden\">\n    <p>Advertisement</p>\n</div>\n<a class=\"visually-hidden skip-to-text-link\" href=\"#story-continues-25\">Continue reading the main story</a>\n</div>\n<p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"102\" data-total-count=\"14496\" id=\"story-continues-25\">A decision to go after an adversary\u2019s launch ability can have unintended consequences, experts warn.</p><p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"471\" data-total-count=\"14967\">Once the United States uses cyberweapons against nuclear launch systems \u2014 even in a threatening state like North Korea \u2014 Russia and China may feel free to do the same, targeting fields of American missiles. Some strategists argue that all nuclear systems should be off limits for cyberattack. Otherwise, if a nuclear power thought it could secretly disable an adversary\u2019s atomic controls, it might be more tempted to take the risk of launching a pre-emptive attack.</p><p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"266\" data-total-count=\"15233\">\u201cI understand the urgent threat,\u201d said Amy Zegart, a Stanford University intelligence and cybersecurity expert, who said she had no independent knowledge of the American effort. \u201cBut 30 years from now we may decide it was a very, very dangerous thing to do.\u201d</p><p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"420\" data-total-count=\"15653\">Mr. Trump\u2019s aides say everything is on the table. China recently <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/2017/02/24/world/asia/china-north-korea-relations-kim-jong-un.html\">cut off coal imports</a> from the North, but the United States is also looking at ways to freeze the Kim family\u2019s assets, some of which are believed held in Chinese-controlled banks. The Chinese have already <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/2016/07/08/world/asia/south-korea-and-us-agree-to-deploy-missile-defense-system.html\">opposed the deployment</a> of a high-altitude missile defense system known as Thaad in South Korea; the Trump team may call for even more such systems.</p><div id=\"story-ad--aggro-40\" class=\"story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent  ad-aggro-4 ad-aggro-8 ad-aggro-10 aggro-only\">\n    <div class=\"accessibility-ad-header visually-hidden\">\n    <p>Advertisement</p>\n</div>\n<a class=\"visually-hidden skip-to-text-link\" href=\"#story-continues-26\">Continue reading the main story</a>\n</div>\n<p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"422\" data-total-count=\"16075\" id=\"story-continues-26\">The White House is also looking at pre-emptive military strike options, a senior Trump administration official said, though the challenge is huge given the country\u2019s mountainous terrain and deep tunnels and bunkers. Putting American tactical nuclear weapons back in South Korea \u2014 they were withdrawn a quarter-century ago \u2014 is also under consideration, even if that step could accelerate an arms race with the North.</p><button class=\"button comments-button  theme-speech-bubble-large\" data-skip-to-para-id=\"\">\n</button>\n<p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"136\" data-total-count=\"16211\">Mr. Trump\u2019s \u201cIt won\u2019t happen!\u201d post on Twitter about the North\u2019s ICBM threat suggests a larger confrontation could be looming.</p><p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"261\" data-total-count=\"16472\">\u201cRegardless of Trump\u2019s actual intentions,\u201d James M.
