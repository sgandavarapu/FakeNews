href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/2017/03/07/upshot/why-even-some-republicans-are-rejecting-the-replacement-bill.html?hp&action=click&pgtype=Homepage&clickSource=story-heading&module=first-column-region&region=top-news&WT.nav=top-news\">recent Republican replacement proposal</a> use refundable tax credits to help people buy their health insurance. That is part of the reason the new G.O.P. bill is <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/2017/03/07/us/politics/affordable-care-act-obama-care-health.html?hp&action=click&pgtype=Homepage&clickSource=story-heading&module=first-column-region&region=top-news&WT.nav=top-news\">under fire from conservatives</a>, who see it as a new entitlement program.</p>\r\n\r\n    </div>\r\n\r\n    \r\n\r\n    \r\n      <div class=\"g-item g-text \">\r\n    \r\n\r\n      <p class=\"g-body\">But the structure of the tax credits is really different. Obamacare calculated the credits based on the cost of insurance in a given area and how much the purchaser could afford to pay. The Republican plan hands out tax credits on a flat basis, according to age. (Both plans cut off subsidies at a certain income level, on the assumption that high earners can pay their own way.) That means that the government subsidy you might get under the different plans would depend on a number of factors \u2013 age, income, address.</p>\r\n\r\n    </div>\r\n\r\n    \r\n\r\n    \r\n      <div class=\"g-item g-text \">\r\n    \r\n\r\n      <p class=\"g-body\">Analysts at the <a href=\"http://kff.org/\">Kaiser Family Foundation</a> <a href=\"http://kff.org/interactive/tax-credits-under-the-affordable-care-act-vs-replacement-proposal-interactive-map/\">estimated these subsidies</a> for every county in the United States across these three variables. The result, shown in the maps above, reveals stark contrasts in federal support, particularly for lower-income Americans.</p>\r\n\r\n    </div>\r\n\r\n    \r\n\r\n    \r\n      <div class=\"g-item g-text \">\r\n    \r\n\r\n      <p class=\"g-body\"><a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/2017/03/06/upshot/gop-health-bill-would-cut-funding-for-poor-and-taxes-on-rich.html?rref=collection%2Fbyline%2Fmargot-sanger-katz&action=click&contentCollection=undefined&region=stream&module=stream_unit&version=latest&contentPlacement=2&pgtype=collection\">The biggest losers</a> under the change would be older Americans with low incomes who live in high-cost areas. Those are the people who <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/2017/01/13/upshot/the-biggest-changes-obamacare-made-and-those-that-may-disappear.html\">benefited</a> <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2014/10/29/upshot/obamacare-who-was-helped-most.html\">most</a> from Obamacare.</p>\r\n\r\n    </div>\r\n\r\n    \r\n\r\n    \r\n      <div class=\"g-item g-text \">\r\n    \r\n\r\n      <p class=\"g-body\">For some people, the new tax credit system will be more generous. The winners are likely to be younger, earn higher incomes and live in areas where the cost of health insurance is low.</p>\r\n\r\n    </div>\r\n\r\n    \r\n\r\n    \r\n      <div class=\"g-item g-text \">\r\n    \r\n\r\n      <p class=\"g-body\">Obamacare's subsidies were structured to limit how much low- and middle-income Americans could be asked to pay for health insurance. Under the G.O.P. proposal, many of the people whose tax credits would fall sharply would be likely to end up uninsured. For people with few resources, a gap of several thousands of dollars between their tax credit and the cost of coverage would be impossible to make up.</p>\r\n\r\n    </div>\r\n\r\n    \r\n\r\n    \r\n      <div class=\"g-item g-text \">\r\n    \r\n\r\n      <p class=\"g-body\">That's why many policy experts believe that the new system would result in fewer Americans having health insurance.</p>\r\n\r\n    </div>\r\n\r\n    \r\n\r\n    \r\n      <div class=\"g-item g-text \">\r\n    \r\n\r\n      <p class=\"g-body\">But if you put Obamacare out of your mind, this program still represents a very large outlay of federal money to help Americans buy health insurance. Before Obamacare passed, those buying their own insurance did not receive government subsidies. And the tax credits in the Republican bill would go to people regardless of whether they even paid taxes -- another reason conservatives oppose it.</p>\r\n\r\n    </div>\r\n\r\n    \r\n\r\n    \r\n Under the G.O.P. proposal, many of the people whose tax credits would fall sharply would be likely to end up uninsured.
