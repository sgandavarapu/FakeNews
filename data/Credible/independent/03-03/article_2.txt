has voted to end visa-free travel for Americans within the EU.</p>\n\n<p>It comes after the US failed to agree visa-free travel for citizens of five EU countries \u2013 Bulgaria, Croatia, Cyprus, Poland and Romania \u2013 as part of a reciprocity agreement. US citizens can normally travel to all countries in the bloc without a visa.</p>\n\n<p>The vote urges the revocation of the scheme within two months, meaning Americans will have to apply for extra documents for 12 months after the <a href=\"http://www.independent.co.uk/topic/european-commission\">European Commission</a> implements a \u201cdelegated act\u201d to bring the change into effect.</p>\n\n<p>The Commission discovered three years ago that the US was not meeting its obligations under the reciprocity agreement but has not yet taken any legal action. The latest vote, prepared by the civil liberties committee and approved by a plenary session of\u00a0parliament, gives the Commission two months to act before MEPs can consider action in the European Court of Justice.</p>\n<script src=\"//player.performgroup.com/eplayer.js#52dfa1298c6a7de209bdffd801.1ji8z9v1roilb1s0kau2cyl6se$videoid=lng28npj6viq1q3ktvl5aozrj&amp;dfchid=50047adlyw0a1c2tuvh34bdwh\" European Union have the chance to object to the \u201cdelegated act\u201d it uses to do so.</p>\n\n<p>In December, MEPs pressed for the move in order to \u201cencourage\u201d Washington to play its part, according to a statement by the parliament.</p>\n\n<p>But Migration Commissioner Dimitris Avramopoulos warned of \u201cconsequences\u201d, including potential \u201cretaliation\u201d and a drop
