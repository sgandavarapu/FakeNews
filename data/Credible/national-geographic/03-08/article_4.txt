Roughly the size of Massachusetts, the Leuser\u2019s 10,000 square miles straddle two provinces, with 85 percent in Aceh and the rest in North Sumatra. The region encompasses Sumatra\u2019s largest intact rain forest and a mix of habitats, from high alpine meadows to peat swamps.</p></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<p>Palm oil\u2014the basis of products such as cosmetics and shampoos, processed foods and biodiesel\u2014is versatile and has a long shelf life.</p></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<p>But oil palm plantations gobble up forest\u2014and not always legally. A <a href=\"http://www.ran.org/leuser_watch\">new report</a> by the NGO <a href=\"http://www.ran.org/\">Rainforest Action Network</a> details the illegal razing of lowland forest, critical habitat for 22 Sumatran elephants, by oil palm grower <i>PT Agra Bumi Niaga</i> (PT ABN). The clearing likely also affects tigers and orangutans that depend on this forest. The Rainforest Action Network is a San Francisco-based NGO with a 30-year history of campaigns targeting major corporate brands implicated in forest destruction, human rights abuses, and climate change pollution.</p></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<p>While the exact population of Sumatran elephants is unknown, they likely number only <a href=\"http://www.iucnredlist.org/details/199856/0\">between 700 and 1,000</a>, according to the International Union for the Conservation of Nature, an environmental network of government and civil society organizations that sets the conservation status of species. It\u2019s estimated that elephant numbers fell by more than 70 percent during the past decade, largely from habitat loss that has squeezed them into smaller areas and cut off their traditional migration routes.</p></div>\n<div class=\"promo section relatedContentList\">      \t\t\n\n\n\n\n      \t\t\n\n\n\n\n      \t\t\n\n\n\n\n\n\n    \n        <div The rest is either forest land with restrictions on use or APL, the acronym denoting land that can be developed. Many areas with the richest biodiversity\u2014the lowland rain forests and peat lands\u2014are outside the national park.</p></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<p>According to the November 2015 <a href=\"http://www.wilmar-international.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Sustainability-Brief-Progress-in-Leuser-Ecosystem_Amend.pdf\">Sustainability Brief</a> of palm oil giant Wilmar International, at least <a href=\"http://www.wilmar-international.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Sustainability-Brief-Progress-in-Leuser-Ecosystem_Amend.pdf\">31 plantation companies</a> (24 local ones and 7 associated with larger parent company groups) operate within the Leuser Ecosystem. Smallholder plantations also cover significant areas, producing about half the region\u2019s palm oil, according to the <a Even the national park itself isn\u2019t immune: The <a href=\"https://programs.wcs.org/indonesia/Wild-Places/Leuser.aspx\">Wildlife Conservation Society</a> reports that Gunung loses more than four square miles a year to illegal logging, oil palm plantations, and expansion of settlements.</p></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<p>\u201cPalm oil plantations have converted 90 percent of prime Sumatran elephant habitat to a monoculture desert,\u201d says <a href=\"http://voices.nationalgeographic.com/2015/09/30/the-forgotten-elephants-of-the-leuser-ecosystem/\">Paul Hilton</a>, a Hong Kong-based photojournalist who focuses on the palm oil issue. \u201cThe future of the critically endangered Sumatran elephant hangs on a thread.\u201d</p></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<h2><b>VIOLATION OF MORATORIUM</b></h2></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<p>Although a majority of buyers of palm oil have pledged to buy only oil produced without further deforestation and harm to the environment, the continued destruction of the Leuser Ecosystem\u2019s fragile areas prompted the national and Aceh governments in April 2016 to agree to a <a href=\"http://www.foresthints.news/minister-and-acehnese-leaders-declare-moratorium-on-palm-oil-and-mining-expansion-in-the-leuser-ecosystem.html\">moratorium</a> on new concessions and on land clearing in existing concessions.</p></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<p>According to <a href=\"http://www.foresthints.news/ministry-seals-off-palm-oil-concession-in-leuser-ecosystem-linked-to-supply-chain-of-top-biofuel-producers\">foresthints.news</a>, which reports on environmental issues, in June 2016 Siti Nurbaya, Indonesia\u2019s minister of the environment and forestry, gave Aceh\u2019s Governor Zaini Abdullah the results of the ministry\u2019s monitoring of the moratorium: PT ABN was still clearing land. So Abdullah sent a circular letter to all plantation companies, including PT ABN, instructing them to stop cutting down forests.</p></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<p>Shortly afterwards the ministry also determined that PT ABN had no valid permit for land-clearing operations in its Leuser concession. A roadblock was put up to prevent PT ABN from accessing the concession, and the ministry told <a href=\"http://www.foresthints.news/ministry-seals-off-palm-oil-concession-in-leuser-ecosystem-linked-to-supply-chain-of-top-biofuel-producers\">foresthints.news</a> that \u201cthe sealing off of concessions represents a legal instrument for protecting the Leuser Ecosystem.\u201d</p></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<p>Yet the Rainforest Action Network\u2019s investigative team\u2014using satellite imagery, aerial drones, and on-the-ground surveillance\u2014found that PT ABN accelerated its clearing from June 2016 through January 2017, bulldozing 800 acres of prime elephant, tiger, and orangutan habitat.</p></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<p>\u201cMonth over month we found active clearance by a company that had its road blocked because of its earlier violation,\u201d says Laurel Sutherlin, the organization\u2019s senior communications strategist and co-author of the report. \u201cFor them to do it again is a scandal.\u201d</p></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<p>A request for comment from PT ABN was made through its website, but the company didn\u2019t respond.</p></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<h2><b>CONFLICTING SIGNALS</b></h2></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<p>It\u2019s unclear whether or how the Indonesian government or the Aceh government will react to violations like PT ABN\u2019s. Neither the environment ministry nor the office of the Aceh governor has responded to requests for comment.</p></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<p>Recent history raises questions regarding the Aceh government\u2019s commitment to protecting the integrity of the Leuser Ecosystem. For example, in 2013 Aceh issued a \u201cspatial\u201d land use plan that allowed for more development. The plan does not recognize the Leuser\u2019s legal status as a National Strategic Area\u2014status that prohibits activities like cultivation and the building of roads and other infrastructure that would degrade the ecosystem\u2019s provision of \u201cservices\u201d such as clean water.</p></div>\n<div class=\"image parbase section\">\n      \t\t\n\n\n\n\n      \t\t\n\n\n\n\n\n According to <a href=\"https://news.mongabay.com/2016/08/aceh-governor-eyes-geothermal-project-in-indonesias-leuser-ecosystem/\">Mongabay.com</a>, in late 2016 Governor Abdullah supported a proposal by a Turkish energy company to build a massive geothermal plant in the heart of the ecosystem and asked Indonesia\u2019s environment and forestry ministry to rezone almost 30 square miles to accommodate its development.</p></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<p>Will such conflicting signals continue now that Aceh has a new governor, Irwandi Yusuf, following elections on February 15? This is Yusuf\u2019s second term. When he was governor from 2007 to 2012, he established a management authority for the Leuser Ecosystem that hired rangers to patrol and prevent illegal hunting and logging.</p></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<p>But according to <a href=\"https://news.mongabay.com/2012/03/palm-oil-case-against-green-governor-in-indonesia-heats-up/\">Mongabay.com</a>, Yusuf also signed, in 2011, a <a href=\"http://www.redd-monitor.org/2011/11/24/irwandi-yusuf-indonesias-green-governor-accused-of-issuing-illegal-palm-oil-concession/\">permit</a> for PT Kallista Alam to convert almost 4,000 acres of peat swamp forest into an oil palm plantation\u2014violating a moratorium on new forest concessions signed three months earlier. (Abdullah later revoked the permit, and following multiple court battles, Kallista Alam was convicted of illegal burning and ordered to pay $26 million in fines.)</p></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<h2><b>\u201cZERO DEFORESTATION\u201d POLICY</b></h2></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<p>During its investigation, the Rainforest Action Network tracked oil palm fruits harvested at PT ABN\u2019s plantation to a nearby processing mill owned by PT Koperasi Prima Jasa (PT KPJ), which in turn sends the crude oil to two refineries owned by Singapore-based Wilmar International.</p></div>\n<div class=\"parbase smartbody section text\">\n<p>Wilmar\u2014the world\u2019s largest palm oil company, controlling more than 45 percent of the global trade\u2014committed in December 2013 to a policy of zero deforestation across all the palm oil it produces, sources, and trades. The <a href=\"http://www.wilmar-international.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/No-Deforestation-No-Peat-No-Exploitation-Policy.pdf\">policy</a> applies to \u201call Wilmar operations worldwide, including those of its subsidiaries, any refinery, mill or plantation that we own, manage, or invest in.\u201d Wilmar supplies oil to companies like PepsiCo, Nestl&eacute;, Procter and Gamble, and
